💡 Analyst Insights
Geopolitical instability around the Strait of Hormuz is accelerating investment in energy and infrastructure, benefiting related resource companies. Samsung Engineering and GS Engineering & Construction are trading higher on expectations of large-scale Middle East pipeline orders exceeding 3 trillion KRW (approximately $2.2 billion). Hyundai Rotem, a supplier of industrial valves and piping materials, is also positioned as an additional beneficiary. U.S.-based energy infrastructure firm Cheniere Energy stands to gain directly from these dynamics. We recommend increasing exposure to defense, construction, and resource stocks in the short term, while monitoring risks from potential export restrictions by Middle Eastern countries or extreme oil price volatility which could trigger market corrections.
