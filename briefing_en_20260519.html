US Treasury Yields, AI Surge, Oil Rally Spotlight Samsung & POSCO

Rising AI investments and crude prices highlight short-term opportunities in Korea’s IT and materials sectors

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Overnight, US major indices showed mixed results with the S&P 500 up 0.38% while Nasdaq declined 0.21%. The materials and technology sectors outperformed, driven by demand in construction-related industries and AI technology. Consumer discretionary and retail stocks fell, pressured by weaker earnings reports. US Treasury yields rose sharply, with the 2-year note hitting a two-year high of 4.92%, signaling tightening financial conditions. Meanwhile, Europe's venture capital market shows stark bifurcation, with AI sectors attracting disproportionate investment.

💡 Analyst Insights
The strongest momentum in US indices came from construction materials and IT infrastructure sectors, which are closely linked to US housing remodel demand and expanded infrastructure spending. Korean companies such as Samsung C&T and POSCO International are poised to benefit directly due to their roles in construction materials and energy resource supply. Globally, Home Depot—reporting $182 billion in annual revenue—reflects robust DIY demand and aging infrastructure replacement trends. We recommend increasing exposure to construction materials and energy-related stocks for short-term gains, while being mindful of risks posed by rising US interest rates and potential consumer demand contraction. Additionally, muted luxury consumption and European rate hikes justify reducing retail sector weightings.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

AI and cloud markets are exceeding growth expectations, with the global AI hardware ecosystem expanding rapidly. South Korean optical and lens technology firms are gaining competitive edge amid this trend, supported by increasing demand in AI hardware markets such as AI glasses. Semiconductor companies like Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix stand to benefit significantly from the shift to AI chips and innovations in development environments. Internationally, firms like Anthropic, Nvidia, and Google accelerate investments and M&A to strengthen AI ecosystem dominance. The global AI market currently surpasses KRW 437 trillion (~$350 billion) with an annual growth rate exceeding 23%.

💡 Analyst Insights
Domestically, semiconductor material and component firms including Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix are positioned as direct beneficiaries of surging AI data center and chip infrastructure demand. Lens and optical part manufacturer LetinAR is emerging as a leader in the AI hardware space, particularly in AI eyewear components. Energy Energy · Industry

International oil prices rebounded strongly, climbing 3.7% last week to $83 per barrel amid renewed energy crisis concerns in the US and Europe and easing of sanctions on Russian oil. Natural gas prices also rose in response to last winter’s severe cold spells, increasing cost pressures on industries. Longer-term fundamentals remain supportive, driven by rising electricity demand in China and India which is propelling growth in renewable energy and power infrastructure sectors.
