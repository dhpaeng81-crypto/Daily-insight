Oil Surges 8%; Major AI and Semiconductor Firms Expand Investments

Geopolitical risks boost energy stocks; AI and chipmakers drive significant investment momentum

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

The US markets closed mixed today with the Dow Jones up 0.7%, Nasdaq down 0.4%, and the S&P 500 nearly flat at +0.2%. Energy and defense sectors led gains, rising 8% and 3% respectively, while real estate and consumer discretionary sectors lagged with declines of 1.2% and 0.8%. The 10-year US Treasury yield rose by 10 basis points to 4.54%, reflecting hawkish Fed signals amid Middle East tensions. The dollar strengthened to 104.6 (+0.6%), contributing to some foreign capital outflows.

💡 Analyst Insights
Energy and defense sectors stand to benefit in the short term due to heightened geopolitical tensions between the US and Iran. Korean trading firms like Samsung C&T and LX International, engaged heavily in commodities, along with defense leaders Hanwha Aerospace and LIG Nex1, should see momentum gains. Globally, British Petroleum is notable for its significant crude oil trading operations amid rising prices. We recommend overweighting energy and defense stocks while reducing exposure to real estate investment trusts and consumer discretionary stocks such as Samsung C&T and Shinsegae Property, which are vulnerable to US consumer spending weaknesses. Key risks include a sharp drop in oil prices that could erode trading margins and prolonged Fed tightening pressures possibly triggering further real estate market corrections.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

Major global AI adopters have escalated large-scale investments and unveiled new technologies. Intel’s stock hit an all-time high, rising 6% driven by increased orders from Apple. AMD is poised for an additional 15% short-term rally fueled by growing demand for AI server chips. OpenAI introduced advanced voice recognition and synthesis via API, accelerating new business applications in mixed reality. The global AI software market is forecasted to surge from KRW 350 trillion in 2024 to KRW 600 trillion by 2026, marking a 71% two-year growth rate. Korean players like Naver Cloud and Hancom are actively entering this expanding market.

💡 Analyst Insights
AI server solutions, semiconductor, and cloud sectors are the prime beneficiaries this week. Domestically, SK Hynix holds a 48% market share in high-bandwidth memory, and SoluM is expanding its server power components supply, both showing rapid earnings improvement. International investors should be mindful of Nvidia’s volatility as the world’s top AI server GPU supplier, but a diversified approach including Intel’s expanding Apple supply and AMD’s new AI accelerator launches remains prudent. Short-term investment calls favor increasing exposure to AI-related hardware and software stocks. Risks include slower-than-expected technological growth, tightening government regulations on algorithms and data privacy, and intensified technological competition from China.
Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices rallied sharply by 8%, surpassing $86 per barrel after direct clashes between US and Iranian forces near the critical Strait of Hormuz, through which over 20% of global maritime oil and LNG shipments pass. Leading oil producers like Russia and Saudi Arabia hinted at further output cuts, intensifying supply concerns. Short-term speculative buying in crude derivatives reached approximately KRW 7 trillion. South Korean refineries and shipping operators posted gains, while renewable sectors like solar and wind underperformed relative to the surge in fossil fuels.

💡 Analyst Insights
The spike in oil prices benefits Korean refiners such as S-Oil and GS, alongside trading firms Samsung C&T and LX International with large commodity portfolios. Conversely, cost pressures are mounting rapidly on energy-intensive sectors like airlines (Korean Air) and chemicals (Lotte Chemical). Globally, major US oil majors ExxonMobil and Chevron are expected to post upward earnings revisions benefiting from higher prices. We advise increasing portfolio weightings in refining and energy trading while reducing stakes in sectors vulnerable to fuel cost inflation such as airlines and chemicals. Investors should watch for geopolitical risk de-escalation, emergency oil releases by Western nations, or demand shocks that could trigger a sharp reversal in oil prices.
