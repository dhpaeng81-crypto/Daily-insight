Bitcoin Hits $68,000, Highest Since January; Gold, Silver Surge

Commodity rally drives asset rotation toward alternatives and defensive sectors

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

As of May 6, 2026, Bitcoin surged to $68,000, marking its highest level since January, alongside a strong rally in Ethereum. Precious metals also gained sharply, with gold rising 2.1% to $2,350 per ounce and silver advancing 3.5% to $29.90 per ounce. Korean stock markets saw strength in defensive and resource sectors due to rising commodity prices, while growth stocks experienced some profit-taking. Global bond yields edged up slightly, with the U.S. 10-year Treasury yield rising 7 basis points to 4.59%, reflecting inflation concerns tied to commodity prices.

💡 Analyst Insights
The simultaneous rise in Bitcoin, gold, and silver is accelerating capital flows into alternative assets globally. In South Korea, ETFs focused on physical gold such as Samsung KODEX Gold Futures and KBSTAR Gold Futures stand to benefit, along with blockchain-related equities like Wemade and Kakao, which holds a stake in Dunamu. Internationally, MicroStrategy continues to expand its Bitcoin holdings, acting as a direct beneficiary, while Newmont gains from the gold price upswing. We recommend increasing portfolio allocations toward gold and Bitcoin over the next one month to capitalize on momentum. However, investors should remain cautious of potential downside risks from further interest rate hikes by the Federal Reserve and tightening regulations on virtual assets.

Energy Energy · Industry

International oil prices declined by nearly 10% over the past week, retreating to approximately $77 per barrel. This drop has prompted lower fixed mortgage interest rates in the U.S., potentially stimulating domestic real estate investment. Meanwhile, renewable energy momentum grows due to increased investments in China-Central Asia and rising European LNG production, supporting green hydrogen and sustainable energy initiatives. Lower energy costs are gradually easing expenses in sectors like transportation, materials, and manufacturing.
