Oil Surges 5% and AI Layoffs Shift Focus to Energy and IT Sectors

Energy and AI sectors gain on oil jump and workforce optimization; financials remain cautious.

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

US major indices showed mixed performance with Nasdaq rising 0.3%, S&P 500 broadly flat, and the Dow declining by 0.1%, highlighting sector variation. Energy stocks rallied sharply, gaining over 6%, fueled by a 5% surge in crude oil prices, which breached $85 per barrel on geopolitical tensions. Semiconductor and material sectors advanced approximately 4%, boosted by NVIDIA and Corning’s factory investments. Conversely, mortgage finance-related stocks declined nearly 2%, pressured by rising interest rate concerns. Overall, the rebound in raw materials prices combined with US-Iran conflicts is elevating market uncertainty for risk assets.

💡 Analyst Insights
The energy and semiconductor material sectors currently warrant overweight positions due to clear short-term tailwinds. Leading Korean beneficiaries include S-Oil, which posted a 22% year-on-year increase in Q1 operating profit sustained by widened refining margins, and Posco Future M, a key player in battery and semiconductor materials benefiting from Corning’s global expansion. On the global front, NVIDIA stands out, spearheading semiconductor ecosystem growth via direct factory investments. Investors are advised to accumulate energy (refining and LNG) and semiconductor material stocks while adopting a cautious stance on home finance-related shares. However, risks such as easing US-Iran tensions or US consumer sentiment weakening may trigger a retracement of recent gains, underscoring the need for profit-taking strategies.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI-driven automation and workforce optimization trend accelerated as prominent US IT firms Cloudflare and Block undertook layoffs representing 5-8% of their workforces. Despite these reductions, Cloudflare posted a 32% year-over-year revenue increase, and Block exceeded performance benchmarks in Q1. Intel’s stock price surged an extraordinary 490% over the past year, reflecting strong market optimism about an IT sector turnaround. In Korea, AI, semiconductor, and cloud security industries are projected to sustain an average annual revenue growth of 18% through 2025, signaling robust mid-term growth potential.

Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices jumped over 5%, reaching above $85 per barrel, driven by escalated US-Iran tensions and Iran’s tightened maritime blockade. Royal Dutch Shell announced an approximately $6.9 billion quarterly profit, exemplifying strong performance among major energy firms. LNG and refining prices also rose an additional 3-4%, exacerbating supply-demand imbalances across Europe and Asia. Geopolitical risks linked to the US, Iran, and Venezuela raise the likelihood of short-term supply disruptions impacting global energy markets.
