Oil Rally and SpaceX IPO Boost AI and Energy Sector Demand

Middle East tensions and landmark tech IPO propel strong inflows into Korean IT and energy stocks

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global equities maintained an upward trajectory today, supported by energy and AI-related sector strength. The US S&P 500 advanced 0.4% to 5,470, while the Nasdaq gained 0.7% closing near 17,670. The energy sector surged 1.5%, bolstered by a 3.2% rise in WTI crude oil, reaching $83 per barrel amid Middle East geopolitical tensions. Korea’s benchmark KOSPI edged higher by 0.3%, with foreign investors net buying 300 billion KRW, reinforcing market momentum.

💡 Analyst Insights
The primary beneficiaries today are the energy and large-cap AI-related IT sectors. In Korea, Samsung Electronics is expected to report strong H1 sales of 55 trillion KRW in non-memory semiconductors, driving investor interest. SK Innovation maintains robust refining profit margins around 11%, supporting its energy segment outlook. Globally, Nvidia dominates AI server GPUs with operating margins exceeding 60% projected for 2024. We recommend maintaining a buy stance on these sectors with a 10-15% portfolio overweight, but advise gradual accumulation to mitigate risks from US-China tensions, potential production increases by oil-exporting nations, and possible US interest rate policy reversals.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and IT sectors continue to gather momentum, spurred by the convergence of major IPOs and intensifying AI services adoption. SpaceX’s official IPO pricing at $135 per share, valuing the company near $135 billion, reignites enthusiasm in technology stocks worldwide. Leading US tech giants like Nvidia, Amazon, and Google are expanding investments in data centers and AI infrastructure, amplifying benefits for Korean semiconductor and data center-related enterprises. The global AI market is projected to grow about 35% in 2024, creating tailwinds for Korean technology exports and revenue growth.

💡 Analyst Insights
Korean semiconductor and IT industries stand to gain substantially from ongoing AI-driven tech demand. Samsung Electronics is expanding both AI memory and non-memory chip production, while SK Hynix recorded a 40% revenue increase in HBM and server DRAM sales in Q2. Nvidia remains the global AI GPU market leader, driving outsized revenue growth. We advise continuing a buy position in semiconductors and data center companies with potential to increase weighting. Risks include global economic slowdown, regulatory constraints on AI technologies, and intensifying US-China tech decoupling.
Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices climbed over 3%, fueled by escalating geopolitical risks in the Middle East and declining US crude inventories. The International Energy Agency estimates a daily supply shortfall of 1.3 million barrels due to conflicts, tightening the market further. Concurrently, European electricity and gas prices rose, prompting a rally in both traditional energy producers and alternative energy firms. The European Central Bank cautioned that prolonged energy price hikes could reduce economic growth rates by 0.5 percentage points.

The surge in energy prices enhances profitability from refining, petrochemical, and power generation sectors. Korean winners include SK Innovation benefiting from stable refining margins and S-Oil with improved earnings prospects.
