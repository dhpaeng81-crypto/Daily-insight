Data Center Power Demand to Quadruple by 2035, Focused Winners Emerge

AI-driven data center growth boosts power, IT, and semiconductor sectors

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global equity markets showed mixed performance today amid weakening expectations for a US interest rate hike delay and fund shifts toward high-dividend and defensive stocks. The US S&P 500 rose 0.5%, while the Nasdaq declined by 0.2%. Europe’s IBEX35 index advanced 0.6%, benefiting from Spain’s foreign investment policy contrasting US restrictions. In real estate, population shifts in the US are supporting land value increases in southern regions and senior housing assets. Domestically in Korea, capital flows favored stable dividend large caps alongside telecom and power utilities.

💡 Analyst Insights
The current macro environment, characterized by persistent high interest rates and easing inflation, favors high-dividend, defensive, and energy sectors for near-term gains. Korea Electric Power Corporation (KEPCO) and SK Innovation stand out as direct beneficiaries of rising crude prices and surging electricity demand. Additionally, Samsung C&T is poised for potential gains from Spain’s liberalization of infrastructure investments. Overseas, Red Electrica, Spain’s national power grid operator, is another key beneficiary. We recommend a short-term overweight stance on these utility and energy stocks while remaining cautious about a hawkish Fed pivot that could pressure equities and revive bond markets.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

AI and data center industries are set to drive a fourfold increase in global electricity consumption by 2035. Over the past two years, the semiconductor, cloud, and power IT markets linked to data centers have grown at an annual rate exceeding 15%. Google recently launched new Gemini AI models, accelerating intelligent service diversification. Leading firms like OpenAI and Apple are reinforcing this trend, propelling substantial investments in Korea’s information networks and infrastructure. Korean IT giants and semiconductor manufacturers have increased AI-related capital expenditures by more than 25% year-over-year, with data center equipment, servers, AI chips, and power automation firms positioned as direct beneficiaries.

💡 Analyst Insights
The expanding power consumption profiles of data centers and AI workloads directly enhance earnings prospects for Korean semiconductor leaders Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix. Infrastructure-oriented companies such as CJ Logistics and SK Broadband also stand to gain through increased data handling needs. Globally, Alphabet (Google) benefits from its integrated AI-hardware ecosystem and infrastructure dominance. We advise increasing allocations to AI and data center-related stocks over the next 3 to 6 months but caution investors about potential delays in AI model commercialization and regulatory risks from heightened energy taxation or greenhouse gas limitations that could trigger tech sector volatility.
Energy Energy · Industry

Oil markets remain tense as traffic through the Strait of Hormuz has stalled, pushing Brent crude prices above $86 per barrel with a 6% rise in the past week. The surge in data center power consumption is expected to increase fuel demand for power plants by about 8% annually, boosting shares of electricity providers such as KEPCO. Meanwhile, major cities in the US and Europe are steadily expanding solar and renewable infrastructures, supporting energy sector investments. Geopolitical risks remain elevated due to ongoing Ukraine-Russia conflict causing perturbations in power and fuel supplies, increasing industry uncertainty.
