💡 Analyst Insights
The semiconductor and AI sectors remain the primary market drivers, underscored by Samsung Electronics’ operating profit explosion from 1.4 trillion KRW to 26.6 trillion KRW year-on-year in Q2, reflecting a 19-fold increase. SK Hynix’s landmark $35 billion IPO in the US further spotlighted Korea’s semiconductor industry on the global stage. US peer Micron Technology is also showing strength, benefiting from supply chain stabilization and market share gains. We recommend increasing exposure to semiconductor equities in the near term. For mid-term portfolio diversification, consider allocations toward energy and raw materials, given rising prices. Investors should monitor potential risks including renewed Federal Reserve rate hikes and geopolitical tensions stemming from Russia-Ukraine and Iranian conflicts, which could trigger market volatility.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The dual focus on open AI ecosystems and semiconductor competition is intensifying. Samsung’s monumental profit growth paired with SK Hynix’s successful IPO cements Korean leadership in global memory chips. Meanwhile, the US and European markets are rapidly adopting open AI models such as OpenAI and Hugging Face, alongside integrating AI education in academia and industry. This synergy between AI demand and semiconductor supply is expected to accelerate growth for Korean technology firms. AI adoption trends bolster long-term demand for Korea’s semiconductor and software sectors.

Investment interest is rising in Korea’s refining, power generation, and hydrogen sectors as inflationary pressures on energy and raw materials impact markets.

Continued increases in oil and gas prices will directly enhance profitability across refining, chemical, and materials industries.
