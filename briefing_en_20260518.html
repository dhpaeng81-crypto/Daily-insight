Oil Surges 7%, KOSPI Retreats; AI Optical Parts Present Buying Opportunity

US-Iran tensions fuel 7% oil jump, boosting energy and AI growth stock volatility

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

On May 18, the Korean stock market weakened amid escalating US-Iran geopolitical tensions and a sharp 7% rise in international crude oil prices. The KOSPI dropped 1.1%, falling below the 2,600 level as most sectors declined except energy and refining, which posted gains. US futures for Dow, S&P 500, and Nasdaq also trended downward, signaling caution in global risk appetite. Cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin (-3.2%) and Ethereum (-5.0%) experienced sharp declines, reflecting increased investor risk aversion. Additionally, Australia's 2026 budget announcement adds a policy variable impacting asset allocation strategies worldwide.

💡 Analyst Insights
During episodes of sharp oil price increases, capital typically flows into energy, refining, and dividend-yielding stocks for short-term gains. In Korea, SK Innovation and S-Oil stand out as direct beneficiaries of rising crude prices due to their refining operations. Globally, ExxonMobil's strong fundamentals amid higher oil prices enhance its investment appeal. Strategically, investors might consider a short-term buy with profit realization planned within 1-3 months to capitalize on volatility. However, risks include potential US Federal Reserve rate hikes or renewed US-Iran diplomatic engagement that could trigger an oil price pullback, pressuring refining stocks. A swift reduction in energy sector exposure is advisable if geopolitical tensions ease or US strategic petroleum reserves are released.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI, data infrastructure, and high-performance optical component industries are experiencing robust growth. South Korean startup LetinAR has entered the AI glasses market supplying miniature lenses the size of thumbnails, a critical optical component. The global AR glasses market is projected to grow annually by 38% through 2026, reaching approximately 8.5 trillion KRW (about $6.8 billion). Major tech firms worldwide including Google, Robinhood, and Apple are aggressively investing in AI data centers and infrastructure, ramping up talent and service sophistication. This growth trajectory bodes well for Korean IT, technology parts, and secondary battery segments poised to benefit from AI integration.

Energy Energy · Industry

International crude oil prices surged nearly 7% in a single day due to heightened US-Iran tensions, pushing Brent crude close to $90 per barrel. The recently commenced Alaska Pikka oil project may boost medium- to long-term supply, but short-term geopolitical uncertainty currently dominates price dynamics. Reflecting this, domestic prices for electricity, gas, and petroleum products in South Korea face upward pressure. Elevated oil prices are expected to accelerate demand for renewable energy technologies and energy-efficiency IT applications that reduce reliance on conventional fuels.
