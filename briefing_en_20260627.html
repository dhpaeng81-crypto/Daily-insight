Meta Platforms, Oil Rally, and OpenAI Chip Race Shape Market Outlook

AI chip independence, rising oil prices, and big tech undervaluation create simultaneous investment opportunities.

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global markets saw a tech-led rally with major US indices gaining between 0.8% and 1.4%, supported by a wave of bargain hunting in big tech stocks. Meta Platforms’ forward 12-month P/E ratio declined to the low 20s, highlighting attractive valuation levels. The bond market responded positively to dovish Fed remarks, with US 10-year Treasury yields falling by 3 basis points, while Korean won-denominated bonds showed steady strength. Sector gains were most pronounced in AI, data centers, and petrochemicals. The Korean market also advanced about 1.5%, driven by IT and secondary battery sectors amid foreign capital inflows.

Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI chip self-sufficiency race is intensifying, exemplified by OpenAI's announcement of 'Jalapeño,' a chip specifically designed for AI inference workloads. Nvidia’s dominance, projected to capture about 80% market share by 2025, is being challenged as new entrants emerge. The AI-dedicated data center market is expanding rapidly at an annual growth rate near 35%, with expectations to reach around 100 trillion KRW (approximately $80 billion) by 2027. Korean companies are actively enhancing their semiconductor value chains, attracting significant investment in AI chip and related technologies.

Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices surged over 3.2% in a single day, reclaiming $80 per barrel following reports of an attack on a tanker off the Omani coast. Additional EU sanctions on Russian energy exports and shrinking US inventory levels are expected to sustain upward pressure on crude prices. Concurrently, natural gas and refined product prices have risen between 2% to 4%. Rising electricity costs and raw material prices suggest widening cost disparities across domestic industrial sectors in Korea.
