OPEC+ Eases Output Cuts, Boosting Korean Refining and Chemicals

💡 Analyst Insights
Lower energy costs benefit Korea’s refining sector, notably S-Oil and SK Innovation, and chemical companies like LG Chem. S-Oil's operating margin is expected to improve from 6% in Q1 to 8% in Q2 on lower crude input costs coupled with steady demand. Globally, ExxonMobil anticipates a 9% increase in Q2 net income with a 5% stock rebound driven by recovering oil product margins. Investors should consider increasing exposure to these sectors over the next three months. However, risks include a possible re-tightening of OPEC+ cuts or renewed Middle East geopolitical tensions that could push prices up and hurt margins.

Energy Energy · Industry

OPEC+’s decision to modestly raise combined monthly oil output by seven member countries, alongside a shift of some tanker routes through Oman-side Hormuz, led Brent crude to drop $0.7 to $84.5 per barrel. Natural gas prices declined 2%, while electricity markets remained stable despite increased cooling demand. In Texas, surging power consumption at data centers fueled a 6% rally in local utilities’ stock prices. Renewable energy and new energy industries are currently in a short-term pause in growth expectations.
