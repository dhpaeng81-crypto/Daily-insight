Hormuz Crisis Spurs Energy Rally, AI Data Center Stocks Lead Gains

Energy supply risks and AI data center investments drive market momentum

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Major global markets showed concurrent strength today, with Korea's KOSPI rising 0.6% and the Nasdaq gaining 0.9%, fueled by robust technology sector performance. US 10-year Treasury yields climbed 4 basis points to 4.31%, reflecting a short-term bond market adjustment amid risk sentiment shifts. Technology and infrastructure-related stocks led gains, while the energy sector faced volatility due to heightened geopolitical tensions around the Strait of Hormuz. Overall, a cautious risk-on mode is visible as investors weigh growth prospects against supply chain uncertainties.

Tech AI · Tech Trends

The technology rally is driven by accelerated investments in AI data center infrastructure and the proliferation of Asian AI model platforms circumventing US export restrictions. The global AI market is projected to expand by 34% in 2024 to approximately 320 trillion KRW. Notably, Apple is experiencing leadership shifts, with a top executive moving to OpenAI, signaling intensifying innovation competition. These dynamics bode well for Korean industries in system semiconductors, cloud services, and power consumption sectors, which are increasingly interconnected as AI deployment expands.

Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices surged past $84 per barrel, rising 4.5% in just one week, driven by attacks on tankers in the Strait of Hormuz and ongoing strikes in Norwegian offshore oil fields. In power markets, renewable output suddenly declined in regions like the UK and Australia, causing power prices to spike by 25% in Australia and triggering battery discharge issues. The Norwegian labor dispute threatens global oil supply continuity, sharpening supply chain tensions. Investors should anticipate increased short-term volatility in gas and power prices alongside sustained expansion in renewable energy investments.
