Oil price plunge reduces cost pressures, driving defensive sector buying in Korea

Fusion energy sector secured $1.3 billion in new funding, while crypto wallet services are expanding rapidly, highlighting volatility in alternative assets. 💡 Analyst Insights
The sharp oil price decline combined with fragile U.S. economic indicators is channeling investor demand toward defensive stocks and low-cost domestic industries in Korea. Within Korea, companies like Daehan Electric Wire (benefiting from lower raw material prices) and SK Innovation (notably refining with reduced input costs) stand out. Globally, NextEra Energy is a key beneficiary with its expanded clean energy investments. We recommend overweighting defensive sectors such as utilities and retail for the short term, with a medium-term focus on energy efficiency-related firms. Potential risks include further U.S. consumer weakness in the housing market and geopolitical tensions between the U.S. and China or additional OPEC production cuts, which could curtail any market rallies.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

Volatility is increasing in technology sectors, led by renewed interest in AI semiconductors and server infrastructure. Despite Nvidia's $27 trillion partnership collapse, AI chip startup Groq successfully raised an additional $850 million, signaling robust capital flow into AI hardware. Taiwan's ecosystem—from data centers to semiconductor packaging—has cemented its importance in global AI infrastructure supply chains. In South Korea, new investment is flowing into semiconductors, data center equipment, and power/cooling solutions industries, poised for growth amid expanding AI infrastructure demands.

💡 Analyst Insights
The expanding AI infrastructure and demand for advanced semiconductors strongly favor Korean firms specializing in these areas. Samsung Electronics (providing high bandwidth memory and AI servers) and Gaon Chips (a semiconductor design specialist) are prime beneficiaries. Globally, Nvidia’s dominance in AI graphic chips remains a key factor. Investment strategy favors increasing exposure to leading domestic semiconductor stocks in the near term. Nonetheless, investors should monitor risks including AI hype-driven overvaluation, short-lived investor exuberance, heightened competition between tech giants and startups, and regulatory or oversupply concerns stemming from U.S. policies.
Energy Energy · Industry

As of mid-May, crude oil prices have dropped 5% over two months, trading near $77 per barrel. Natural gas saw a modest rebound of 1.67%, while heating oil and gasoline prices continued to slide. Globally, enhanced EU regulations on building energy efficiency and consolidation moves among large U.S. power companies underscore momentum toward energy restructuring. Controversies regarding tax avoidance by major energy firms are also fuelling investor uncertainty in the sector.

The oil price decline is exerting a mid- to short-term cost reduction effect across petrochemical, materials, aviation, and transportation industries. In Korea, LG Chem benefits from cheaper petrochemical feedstocks, while Korean Air gains from lower jet fuel expenses.
