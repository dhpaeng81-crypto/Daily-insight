Memory Stocks Retreat Amid AI Selling; Energy Volatility Surges Oil Prices

Memory prices dip; AI and energy sectors face profit-taking and volatility shifts.

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global markets showed heightened volatility today. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index fell 2.1% due to concerns over weakening memory chip prices, dragging down AI and IT-related stocks. The S&P 500 declined 0.4%, while South Korea’s KOSPI closed down 0.8% amid net foreign selling pressure. Defensive sectors like secondary batteries and defense stocks exhibited resilience. Meanwhile, energy and commodity prices surged on rising geopolitical uncertainties, contributing to increased market swings.

💡 Analyst Insights
The AI and memory semiconductor sectors are clearly entering a short-term correction phase. Leading Korean players Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix maintain strong Q2 earnings expectations, yet North American memory prices declined 2-3% in June, reflecting softening demand. Nvidia also underperformed globally amid supply chain concerns and valuation pressures. Investors should adopt a neutral stance in the near term, focusing on profit-taking around peak valuations but consider medium-term overweighting based on earnings growth potential. Key downside risks include a slowdown in AI demand growth, memory oversupply, and geopolitical tensions dampening market sentiment.

Energy Energy · Industry

Energy markets experienced substantial volatility driven by fresh geopolitical tensions between Iran and Israel, and Europe's sanctions on Russian energy supplies. West Texas Intermediate crude briefly surged above $85 before retreating to around $83 per barrel. Natural gas prices fluctuated amid news of resumed Russia-China pipeline operations, while refinery margins soared due to disrupted oil transport routes. These supply chain bottlenecks are poised to directly influence profitability for domestic Korean refiners and gas companies.
