Oil Weakness & AI Infrastructure Surge Benefit Samsung C&T and More

Oil down 5%, AI data center investments soar; Korean construction and IT stocks gain.

Comcast jumped 6% following a corporate split announcement. Despite a boom in AI-related mergers and acquisitions, private equity firms face exit challenges, potentially slowing growth. Globally, $2 trillion in stock buybacks are underway as capital reallocates towards AI infrastructure themes, mirrored by foreign net buying in Korea’s AI, infrastructure, construction, and materials sectors.

💡 Analyst Insights
The expansion of AI infrastructure and data center investments is delivering near-term benefits to construction and materials sectors in both the US and Korea. Samsung C&T and POSCO Holdings are expected to see 2Q operating profit growth of 11% and 8% year-over-year, respectively, on new data center construction and elevated steel and plant demand. Globally, Caterpillar’s heavy machinery sales for data centers pushed its valuation above $210 billion with further 5% upside projected. We recommend increasing exposure to data center and plant-related stocks in the short term. Key risks include a sudden AI bubble correction or a North American economic slowdown delaying investment commitments, so phased profit-taking is prudent.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and IT markets are rapidly evolving with new open-source AI tools and ultra-fast, low-cost image generators. Nvidia’s main competitor, Etched, has surpassed $5 billion valuation and secured $1 billion in AI chip contracts. Anthropic launched Claude Sonnet 5, offering 30% lower operational costs and expanded applicability in software and finance. Google improved its image generation tool with up to 40% cost savings and 20% faster response times, strengthening its creator market dominance. In Korea, capital flows focus heavily on data centers, cloud infrastructure, and AI semiconductor value chains.

Energy Energy · Industry

International oil prices declined 5% to $69 per barrel on June 30, stabilizing at a weak level despite regional Iran-Israel tensions. Renewable energy investments are rising globally, with a projected 8% increase to $325 billion in 2024. Major oil companies, such as BP, are undergoing management changes and restructuring amid shifting energy policies. Concerns arise over potential US offshore wind support cuts under possible policy shifts. Natural gas inventories in Eastern and Western Europe dropped to their lowest in 15 years, indicating short-term supply risks. In South Korea, electricity and gas tariffs are likely to increase further within the year.
