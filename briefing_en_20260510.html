Nvidia's $40B AI Investment and Rising China Energy Imports Reshape Markets

AI expansion and global energy shifts drive differentiated capital flows in Korea and worldwide.

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global financial markets showed mixed performances as major tech stocks surged while energy costs climbed. The US S&P 500 index increased by 0.4% and the Nasdaq by 0.7%. Meanwhile, South Korea's KOSPI fell 0.3%, pressured by foreign net selling of 120 billion KRW. Utilities and AI infrastructure-related financials led gains with over 2% rises, contrasting with real estate and non-face-to-face consumer sectors declining by more than 1%. The US 10-year treasury yield remained firm near 4.5%, while USD and CNY exchange rates rose 0.2% and 0.5%, influencing emerging market capital flows. Macro conditions are shifting capital towards energy security and AI-driven data center investments.

Tech AI · Tech Trends

Investment in AI is accelerating steeply, highlighted by Nvidia’s $40 billion equity investments in AI-related companies this year. Demand for data centers and semiconductors is surging, with high-performance server requirements growing at a 28% annual rate. Korean semiconductor giants Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix reported over 25% year-over-year growth in Q1 operating profit, driven by AI-linked memory shipments. Intel’s stock has soared 490% in 12 months, although scrutiny remains on the sustainability of its improving earnings momentum. The rapid AI adoption is also prompting structural changes in employment and industry landscapes.

Energy Energy · Industry

International crude oil prices rose 2.5%, with Dubai crude reaching $87 per barrel amid escalating tensions around Iran and the Strait of Hormuz. Qatari LNG shipments are increasing, while China's energy imports surged 12% in April, exacerbating global supply-demand imbalances. Energy policies in key producers, such as Alaska’s restructuring of subsidies and distribution, are beginning to impact mid-term electricity and natural gas pricing. In South Korea, rising power tariff pressure and increasing trading volumes in power generation and energy equipment stocks are notable market trends.
