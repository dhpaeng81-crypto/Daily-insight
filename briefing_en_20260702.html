Oil Rises 1.3% as AI Stocks Gain Momentum

Oil rebounds 1.3% while AI platform firms lead sector shifts in global markets

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global financial markets showed mixed performance amid rising volatility. The S&P 500 index gained 0.5%, with the NASDAQ advancing 0.9%, driven by strong tech sector momentum. Conversely, retail and brick-and-mortar stores experienced a significant downturn; the sector index declined 1.4%, pressured by over 1,000 store closures from a leading U.S. retailer. Easing tensions around Iran coupled with AI-related optimism boosted investor sentiment, resulting in noticeable capital flows toward technology stocks.

💡 Analyst Insights
The financial market's primary beneficiaries are AI-related stocks, semiconductors, and renewable energy firms. Samsung Electronics, a global leader in memory semiconductors and a key supplier in NVIDIA’s supply chain, stands to gain substantially from soaring AI demand. LG Innotek, specializing in high-performance automotive components, also expects revenue growth tied to AI expansion. On the global front, NVIDIA leads with an extraordinary 125% annual revenue growth, setting the pace for the semiconductor industry. We recommend increasing exposure to AI and semiconductor sectors while reducing weight in retail and real estate holdings. Investors should remain cautious of potential reversals due to renewed geopolitical risks in Iran or additional U.S. interest rate hikes that could quickly dampen market risk appetite.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and IT sectors are rapidly advancing with accelerated commercialization of AI platform services. Venice AI recently achieved unicorn status with annual revenues exceeding $70 million (approximately 95 billion KRW), reflecting strong market adoption. Google released ‘Gemini Spark,’ a new AI productivity assistant now available on Mac, intensifying platform competition. SpaceX unveiled a prototype AI-enabled device to investors that could expand its footprint into wireless communication, illustrating the convergence of AI, wireless, and aerospace industries. Korean AI-related companies are also benefiting as domestic demand for AI data and platforms grows swiftly, driving stock gains.

Industries poised to profit from AI and IT advances include data centers, semiconductor manufacturers, and platform service providers. SK Hynix, focusing on High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) tailored for AI applications, and Naver, with its proprietary AI services, are expected to see significant improvements in profitability. Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices recovered 1.3% amid eased geopolitical tensions in Iran, with West Texas Intermediate crude reaching $82.60 per barrel. Gold prices declined for a third consecutive day, falling approximately 2%, reflecting diminished safe-haven demand. Electricity prices in the UK and Australia exhibited mixed movements due to policy shifts; Australia introduced free electricity hours for households, leading to observable energy savings. Meanwhile, the U.S. canceled new gas-fired power plant projects, and the UK is grappling with high electricity costs burdening the industrial sector.
