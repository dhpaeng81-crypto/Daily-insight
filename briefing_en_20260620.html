Bitcoin Drops 7% as AI and Energy Stocks Surge Amid Sector Rotation

KOSPI rises 1.2% near 2,770; Nasdaq up 0.9% led by tech; Bitcoin falls below $68,000

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global and Korean stocks rebounded today driven by easing geopolitical tensions. South Korea's KOSPI increased by 1.2%, approaching the 2,770 level, while the US Nasdaq gained 0.9% with technology stocks leading. In contrast, Bitcoin experienced a sharp 7% decline, falling to around $68,000, reflecting broader weakness in cryptocurrencies. Analysts attribute the stock market stability to the continued pause in US interest rate hikes, improved US consumption data, and decreasing geopolitical risks.

💡 Analyst Insights
Technology and energy sectors are currently benefiting from positive market sentiment. Korean companies like Samsung Electronics and EcoPro are poised for earnings growth, supported by expanding AI adoption and rising demand for secondary battery materials. On the global stage, Nvidia's stock has rebounded strongly due to increased AI semiconductor demand and expanding data center investments. We recommend increasing exposure to technology and energy stocks in the short term, while maintaining a cautious 'wait-and-see' approach on Bitcoin and broader crypto assets until clear recovery signs emerge. Investors should monitor potential short-term corrections in large caps resulting from renewed US-China trade tensions or emerging market currency volatility.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The global IT and AI landscape is becoming fiercely competitive, focusing on data center-specific solutions, AI application models, and cloud service platforms. AI inference startup Baseten is nearing a record $1.5 billion funding round at a $13 billion valuation, fueling concerns over a potential valuation bubble. Concurrently, US regulatory scrutiny emerged as the government blocked Anthropic’s release of its newest AI models Fable 5 and Mythos 5 due to national security issues. In Korea, companies such as Naver and POSCO DX continue to benefit from increased AI data center operations and cybersecurity demand, sustaining share price momentum.

💡 Analyst Insights
AI data centers, cybersecurity, and semiconductor design firms are expected to be the key beneficiaries. Korean tech leaders Naver and Hanwha Systems are well-positioned to profit directly from their large-scale AI data center operations and enhanced cyber defense capabilities. Globally, Nvidia maintains its dominant position as the primary supplier of AI chips and essential data center components. Energy Energy · Industry

International crude oil prices declined slightly by 0.7% to $81 per barrel as regional geopolitical risks, particularly US-Iran tensions, eased modestly. Power and gas markets continue under upward pressure due to increased electricity demand from data centers. Renewable energy prospects are brightened by a $900 million US offshore wind tender success, signaling growing global orders. Both Europe and South Korea are aggressively pushing energy mix diversification policies to reduce reliance on traditional fossil fuels.
