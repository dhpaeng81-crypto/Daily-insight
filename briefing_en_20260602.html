Alphabet Secures $80 Billion for Massive AI Infrastructure Expansion

Big tech AI and energy investments accelerate; selective buying in Korean IT and raw materials advised

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

U.S. markets closed mixed overnight, buoyed by gains in energy and AI sectors. The S&P 500 rose 0.3%, Nasdaq gained 0.5%, while the Dow Jones edged down 0.1%. Rising Middle East tensions pushed Brent crude to $93.64 per barrel, up 2.7%, lifting energy stocks. Defensive sectors including healthcare and banking corrected amidst concerns over supply chain and regulatory risks. Notably, major bank reports recommend reducing exposure to healthcare giant Cigna due to PBM transition risks, while value-oriented growth funds target undervalued tech names.

💡 Analyst Insights
Advancements in AI are driving exponential increases in server and data center investments, directly benefiting Korean suppliers. SK Hynix, known for high-bandwidth memory products, and Samsung Electronics, maintaining leadership in high-performance chips, are prime beneficiaries. Infrastructure provider LS ELECTRIC, specializing in ultra-high voltage power equipment, is also favored. Energy Energy · Industry

Heightened Middle East geopolitical tensions have sustained upward pressure on oil prices, with Brent crude closing at $93.64 per barrel, a 2.7% increase. European nations including France escalated sanctions by seizing Russian oil tankers, exacerbating supply uncertainties. Meanwhile, U.S.-Iran conflicts and rising energy demands in Africa add volatility on both supply and demand sides. These dynamics are pushing up prices for oil, LNG, and power, providing renewed momentum for electric infrastructure and data center-related industries that rely heavily on stable energy supplies.

Rising international oil prices are translating into strong profit growth for Korean refiners such as SK Innovation and S-Oil.
