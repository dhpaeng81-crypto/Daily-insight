Energy and AI Led Surge: Samsung C&T and SK E&S Rise Amid US Nuclear and AI Shifts

US nuclear investments and AI layoffs focus Korean investors on select large caps

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global and Korean markets edged higher today supported by steady US 10-year Treasury yields at 4.27%. The Nasdaq climbed 0.6%, led by gains in AI-benefiting tech giants, while the S&P 500 rose 0.4%. Domestic consumer sectors, including foodservice and retail, underperformed amid multiple restaurant closures. Investor capital is increasingly reallocating toward growth industries, reinforced by Federal Reserve rate pause expectations.

💡 Analyst Insights
Investment focus remains on US technology and emerging energy sectors. Samsung C&T (Samsung Corporation & Trading) benefits from expanded large-scale and eco-friendly project investments, while SK E&S is poised for short-term earnings growth through its augmented global LNG and power trading operations. Internationally, Nvidia leads AI semiconductor gains, forecasting a 70% revenue jump in Q2, signaling robust sector momentum. Our recommended investment action is to overweight Samsung C&T with a target price raised to KRW 170,000 and SK E&S to KRW 100,000. Key risks include US consumer spending decline, highlighted by 24 Outback Steakhouse competitor location closures, and a potential global credit squeeze.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The global IT market is undergoing rapid restructuring fueled by AI advancement. Oracle announced workforce cuts of 21,000 jobs this year linked directly to AI automation, reflecting a widespread industry trend. AI-powered enterprise solutions driven by Nvidia, OpenAI, and Anthropic are central to this transformation, with an expected industry growth rate near 25% annually. Korean IT majors, such as Naver and Samsung SDS, are aggressively developing AI-based new businesses to capitalize on these trends.

Energy Energy · Industry

Crude oil prices decreased by about 1% to $80.5 per barrel amid eased tensions near the Strait of Hormuz and stabilized supply conditions. The US announced $17.5 billion in loans for constructing 10 new nuclear reactors, responding to an anticipated 20% rise in electricity demand over the next decade. This reflects a commitment in the US and Europe to strengthen investments in clean and alternative energies, elevating companies specializing in nuclear power and electricity infrastructure to prominence.
