Oil Surges 5%, LG Energy Solution & SK Hynix Gain on AI Data Center Boom

Rising oil prices and AI data center investments drive semiconductor and energy stocks higher

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global financial markets saw a rebound with the US S&P 500 Index rising 1.3%, led by strength in AI and IT sectors. While financials showed mixed performance, growth stocks in biotech and semiconductors outperformed. The US telecom sector gained positively following AT&T's market consolidation after a rival’s bankruptcy, easing credit concerns temporarily. The US Treasury market saw minor yield drops, especially in short-term notes, and the USD-KRW exchange rate stabilized near 1,390 won per dollar. Overall, AI, semiconductor, and telecom infrastructure stocks remain strong in the near term.

💡 Analyst Insights
Semiconductors, particularly data center component suppliers, and telecommunications sectors are the day's biggest winners. SK Hynix benefits from surging demand for high-capacity DRAM used in AI servers, with first-half sales expected to increase by 33%. KT Corporation is poised to expand new business lines driven by pent-up 5G network infrastructure investment. Globally, AT&T's consolidation of market share following a competitor's bankruptcy supports a 4% Q3 revenue growth outlook. Investment actions suggest overweighting SK Hynix and KT, and a short-term buy on AT&T. However, investors should monitor risks from potential US interest rate hikes and geopolitical tensions in the Middle East that could trigger market corrections.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and IT sector experienced major advances with the launch of OpenAI’s GPT-5.6 series and Meta's announcement of a $13 billion Canadian data center. Worldwide AI infrastructure investment is projected to surpass 400 trillion KRW this year, fueling strong demand for next-generation semiconductors and memory products. Leading firms such as Meta and OpenAI are expanding influence over both AI software and hardware infrastructure, boosting growth prospects for Korean semiconductor and data center infrastructure companies. These trends underpin a robust near-term growth outlook for Korean DRAM and SSD makers.

💡 Analyst Insights
Korean companies stand to gain significantly from AI-driven demand surges. Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix are advancing AI server chip solutions, including low-power DRAM and SSDs, while LG Energy Solution is securing 2nd battery contracts worth about 3 trillion KRW annually for AI data centers and power grid applications. Meta's intensive AI infrastructure investment cements its global leadership and sets competitive benchmarks. Recommended investment stance is to overweight Samsung Electronics, SK Hynix, and LG Energy Solution, and to take long positions in Meta. Risks include possible erosion of AI module profitability and increased regulatory scrutiny in the US and Europe that may cause short-term price volatility.
Energy Energy · Industry

Energy markets saw oil prices jump 5%, reaching $85 per barrel, driven by shipping disruptions near the Strait of Hormuz. US natural gas prices edged up slightly due to maintenance shutdowns impacting storage and export facilities. Ongoing strikes on Russian oil and gas infrastructure linked to the Ukraine conflict raise supply concerns, highlighting vulnerabilities in global energy logistics. These dynamics are creating ripple effects across manufacturing, IT, and heavy industries, amplifying global energy supply risks.

Higher oil prices benefit refining and export-oriented companies such as S-Oil and LG Chem, though they pressure air transport and logistics sectors. LG Energy Solution is expected to see increased revenues from energy storage systems and batteries for data center power applications. SK Innovation anticipates improved earnings from high-value refined product exports estimated at approximately 2 trillion KRW annually. Globally, ExxonMobil is positioned to benefit from expanded shale gas production and export opportunities. Tactical investment calls favor overweight positions in S-Oil and LG Energy Solution, with a caution to consider partial profit-taking if oil price tensions ease. Geopolitical volatility in the Middle East and weakening US economic growth remain key downside risks that could trigger sharp energy price corrections.
