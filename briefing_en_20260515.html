China's Surge in US Oil Imports Boosts Energy and Export Stocks

China's large-scale US crude oil buying drives energy and export sector gains, lifting risk appetite.

Emerging markets also saw increased risk appetite; the Shanghai Composite climbed 0.9%, while South Korea’s KOSPI advanced over 1%. The US 10-year Treasury yield slightly declined to 3.48%, supporting inflows totaling more than KRW 903 billion into liquidity-sensitive ETFs. Semiconductors, energy, and large-cap export stocks led the gains amid global economic optimism.

💡 Analyst Insights
The recent rally is primarily driven by rising crude oil prices and China's strategic decision to ramp up US crude oil purchases, stimulating demand for energy and export-related stocks. South Korean companies poised to benefit include SK Innovation, known for its crude oil trading and refining operations, and Samsung Electronics, which is expected to see export growth supported by a stronger global demand environment. On the international front, ExxonMobil stands out as a key beneficiary from increased US oil exports. We recommend overweighting energy and export sectors in the near term and initiating purchases in major ETFs tracking these themes, such as the KODEX Energy and Materials ETF (069500). However, investors should monitor potential volatility from US-China trade negotiations and rising maritime freight costs, which could impact profitability.

Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices surged approximately 3% following news of China’s agreement to purchase substantial volumes of US crude, with Brent crude rebounding to around USD 86 per barrel. Global oil inventories have declined about 2% week-over-week, signaling tightening supply conditions. Leading oil-producing nations, including the US, have indicated intentions to increase production. Meanwhile, geopolitical tensions remain elevated in regions like Cuba and Latvia, where power outages and political instability persist. Renewable energy investments remain limited; fossil fuel demand and transportation logistics continue to be the primary factors influencing near-term price movements.
