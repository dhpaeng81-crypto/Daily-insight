TSMC surges 7% amid energy and AI sector strength

TSMC and oil prices rally, boosting AI and energy sectors; short-term portfolio rotation opportunities widen

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global financial markets showed mixed performances today, with major indices exhibiting divergent trends. Technology stocks staged a strong rebound, led by Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC), which nearly surged 7%, powering gains in the Nasdaq. Meanwhile, the U.S. retail sector faced headwinds as a 69-year-old iconic furniture chain filed for bankruptcy, signaling ongoing structural challenges. Defensive stocks like Linde plc rose by 3%, reflecting investors' cautious optimism. Interest rates and the U.S. dollar index entered a consolidation phase following recent adjustments.

Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and data center markets continue rapid expansion, underpinning semiconductor sector strength. TSMC’s 7% jump highlights escalating long-term AI semiconductor demand, reinforcing positive momentum across the global semiconductor value chain. Major platforms such as Meta and Apple are accelerating AI-based service rollouts, with the AI market expected to surpass KRW 350 trillion (~USD 270 billion) in 2024. This evolution presents significant growth potential for Korea’s large IT conglomerates, telecommunications firms, semiconductor producers, and data center operators.
