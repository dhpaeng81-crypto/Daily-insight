US-Iran Tensions Escalate, Brent Crude Nears $100 per Barrel Again

Geopolitical risks push oil prices higher, increasing volatility for Korean export and import sectors.

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

US stock indices declined around 1.3% amid rising military tensions between the US and Iran. Major indices in the S&P 500 and Dow Jones reflected broad-based weakness, with financials and consumer discretionary sectors leading losses. Conversely, defense and energy stocks showed relative strength as investors sought safer assets amid escalating geopolitical uncertainty. Technology and retail sectors underperformed, continuing a theme of risk-off sentiment. The market environment indicates increased global uncertainty and short-term risk aversion.

💡 Analyst Insights
Geopolitical tensions and rising oil prices are creating near-term beneficiaries in defense and energy sectors. Korean companies such as Hanwha Aerospace are poised to gain from surging defense sales, while SK Innovation stands to benefit from improved earnings in refining and petrochemicals. Globally, defense giant Raytheon Technologies remains an attractive target given its leadership in military technologies and increased US defense budgets. The recommended short-term investment strategy is to overweight defense and refining stocks. However, investors should be prepared for potential quick reversals in asset prices if US-Iran hostilities de-escalate or global oil prices retreat.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

Competition in the AI platform space is intensifying with leading tech giants like Anthropic and Apple accelerating new AI service launches. Both Korean and international companies are increasing investments in generative AI, security AI, and manufacturing data-driven AI, with corporate AI budgets growing 5 to 7 times annually. Rapid advancements are occurring in data center efficiency and tailored AI software development ahead of previous expectations. The global AI market is projected to expand over 20% in 2024, reaching approximately KRW 60 trillion (around USD 50 billion).

💡 Analyst Insights
The AI sector's structural growth is now focused on infrastructure—cloud, semiconductors, and security software industries. Domestically, SK Hynix is positioned to benefit due to its industry-leading High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) DRAM products, and Douzone Bizon is gaining from its ERP-based AI solutions. Globally, Nvidia dominates with over 80% market share in chips used for generative AI applications. Maintaining a buy stance on AI hardware and software stocks is advisable, while monitoring for risks related to declining corporate profitability from rapid budget increases and tightening AI regulations worldwide.
Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices have recently surged toward $100 per barrel driven by renewed geopolitical risks in the Middle East. Major producers such as Saudi Arabia and Russia have emphasized cooperation to stabilize oil markets amid decreasing crude inventories in the US and other key economies. Additionally, momentum is building in the US for nuclear licensing reforms, potentially boosting investment flows into renewables and nuclear power sectors. Global natural gas prices rose by 0.7%, contributing to short-term strength in electricity and gas markets.
