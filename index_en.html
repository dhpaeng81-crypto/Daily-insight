U.S. Expands Nuclear & Clean Energy Investment by $17.5 Billion

Clean energy and nuclear beneficiaries gain amid surging U.S. energy infrastructure spend

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

U.S. equities edged lower due to profit-taking and tech sector adjustments, with the Nasdaq down 0.5% and the S&P 500 slipping 0.3%. The financial and energy sectors outperformed, supported by robust investor interest in safe-haven assets such as U.S. Treasuries. Semiconductor sentiment softened after Intel's CEO tempered expectations, causing selective caution in tech investments. Overall, global capital flows favored defensive plays as geopolitical and macroeconomic uncertainties persist.

💡 Analyst Insights
With the U.S. government intensifying investments in clean energy and nuclear infrastructure, energy and power equipment sectors stand to benefit significantly. Korean companies such as Doosan Enerbility, a major nuclear parts supplier, and Korea Electric Power Corporation (KEPCO), which manages the power grid, are well positioned to capture new orders and operational contracts. On the global front, NextEra Energy remains a leading clean energy stock benefiting from these policies. Our recommendation is to overweight energy and power infrastructure stocks for medium to long-term gains. However, investors should remain cautious of potential short-term volatility from delayed U.S. interest rate cuts and geopolitical risks, especially in the Middle East.

Energy Energy · Industry

The U.S. Department of Energy has authorized $17.5 billion in loans to support new nuclear power projects, injecting momentum into nuclear and clean energy infrastructure development. International crude oil prices rose by about 1%, with West Texas Intermediate reaching $81 per barrel, while electricity and natural gas prices edged slightly higher. Large corporations like Walmart have accelerated their clean energy transition, signing long-term renewable power purchase agreements exemplified by its deal with Constellation Energy. These developments enhance order prospects for related Korean industries.
