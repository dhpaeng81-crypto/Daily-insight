Hormuz Strait Tensions Trigger 7% Swings in Energy Stocks

Middle East geopolitical risks revive energy rally, crude oil soars 5.3% on supply fears

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

On a cautious day, the KOSPI index ended down 0.8% near 2,700 amid global subdued market sentiment. Profit-taking pressured tech and growth stocks, sending the US Nasdaq lower by over 1%. Conversely, energy and defense sectors surged by 3% to 7%, reflecting rising geopolitical concerns. The 10-year US Treasury yield climbed to 4.45%, highlighting bond market weakness. Overall, the market volatility expanded due to Middle East energy supply uncertainties and ongoing US Federal Reserve rate hike apprehensions.

💡 Analyst Insights
Energy and defense sectors currently benefit the most amid short-term geopolitical tensions. Korean Oil Corporation (Korea National Oil Corporation) and Hanwha Aerospace are seeing upward revisions in contracts and earnings forecasts due to potential closure of the Hormuz Strait. Globally, ExxonMobil is positioned to benefit from rising crude prices. The recommended investment stance is to overweight energy and defense stocks to capture near-term gains. However, investors should remain vigilant as easing Middle East tensions or slowing demand in the US and China could trigger swift market corrections.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and IT sectors face a complex adjustment phase driven by talent outflows, privacy challenges, and regulatory uncertainties. The departure of Nobel laureate John Jumper from Google DeepMind and recent concerns about chatbot reliability have dampened global AI investment sentiment. Despite this, Korean AI data center and semiconductor sectors maintain solid annual growth of 15-20%. Market entry barriers are lowering with AI technology becoming mainstream, yet increased governmental and international regulations pose headwinds to innovation momentum.

Energy Energy · Industry

Geopolitical instability centered on the Strait of Hormuz resulted in a sharp 5.3% increase in Dubai crude prices, returning above $88 per barrel within one day. This critical passageway accounts for 20% of global oil shipments, causing 4-7% volatilities in energy shares worldwide. Meanwhile, the SunZia renewable energy project in the US began operation, drawing short-term green energy interest. Additionally, drone strikes on Russian refineries injected further uncertainty into global energy supply chains.
