Buffett's Dividend Stock Exit Spurs $75B AI, Energy Capital Flows

Capital shifts to AI and energy sectors; dividend stocks sold off amid geopolitical tensions

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global financial markets showed distinct capital reallocation today. Warren Buffett’s Berkshire Hathaway sold its entire holding in dividend-focused stocks, dragging down U.S. high-dividend indices like the S&P High Dividend Index by approximately 1.2%. In contrast, infrastructure and energy-related equities such as Keel Infrastructure Corp. (KEEL) and T1 Energy Inc. (TE) surged 3.5% and 4.1%, respectively, driven by heavy new buying activity. Growth-oriented sectors saw less inflow, with the broader major indices trading flat within a 0.3% range. U.S. Treasury yields declined by around 5 basis points, reinforcing a moderate risk-on environment.

💡 Analyst Insights
Infrastructure and energy sectors are the primary beneficiaries of current capital movements. Korean companies Korea Electric Power Corporation’s KPS unit (KEPCO KPS) and SK E&S stand out as domestic proxies, expected to post first-half year-on-year earnings expansions of roughly 12% and 9% respectively, underpinned by expanding power equipment maintenance and renewable energy investments. Globally, Berkshire Hathaway’s exit is accelerating the rotation from traditional dividend and value stocks to infrastructure and growth-oriented AI names. Investors should consider increasing exposure to infrastructure and energy sectors in the near term. However, geopolitical risks, specifically from the Russia-Ukraine conflict, and volatility in U.S. interest rates and dollar strength pose downside risks for sudden market corrections.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and IT sectors are energized by significant investments in data center capacity and AI hardware innovation. SoftBank announced plans to invest up to €75 billion to add 5 gigawatts of new data center supply in France, signaling a major European infrastructure expansion to support AI workloads. Concurrently, debates over AI adoption’s societal impacts, such as job displacement and environmental criticisms of data centers, are intensifying worldwide. Leading global firms Meta and Microsoft continue to expand AI hardware and coding ecosystems. Within Korea, strategic industries like semiconductors, renewable energy, and data centers are positioned for sustained growth amid these technological shifts.

Energy Energy · Industry

Energy markets reacted sharply to the confirmed strike on Russia’s Saratov oil refinery, pushing Brent crude prices up 3.6% to $83.70 per barrel. In Europe, eased taxation on corporate vehicles is expected to temporarily lift petroleum demand, with new cars accounting for 59% of sales and oil consumption comprising 78% of those vehicles. In California, wholesale electricity prices hit record lows, averaging 22 Korean won per kWh, aided by a 10% rise in renewable power generation. Globally, surging AI industry electricity demand highlights the deepening convergence between energy and IT sectors.
