Oil Falls 10%, SK Innovation and S-Oil See Sharp Corrections

US-Iran peace deal triggers energy plunge, benefiting renewables and airlines

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global equity markets reversed earlier losses following news of a US-Iran peace deal. The Dow Jones Industrial Average hit a new intraday high, rising 0.8%, while energy stocks plunged sharply between 4% and 6%, reflecting sector divergence. Crude oil prices dropped 10% within a week, sharply reflecting shifting market sentiment. The US 10-year Treasury yield declined moderately to 4.24%, indicating increased appetite for risk assets.

💡 Analyst Insights
The peace accord has shifted investor preference towards defensive sectors and technology stocks, reducing short-term demand for traditional energy and chemical companies. In South Korea, SK Innovation and S-Oil, key energy players, are expected to face intensified selling pressure, making a short-term sell or reduce recommendation prudent. Conversely, sectors benefiting from lower oil prices — such as renewables, travel, and aviation — are likely to attract inflows. Korean carriers like Korean Air and Jeju Air stand out as likely beneficiaries. On the global stage, United Airlines and Alphabet (Google's parent company) also gain from weakening energy costs. Investors should monitor for indiscriminate selling and watch for potential oil price recovery tied to production adjustments by Saudi Arabia and Russia.

Energy Energy · Industry

International oil prices plunged roughly 10% to just above $80 per barrel following the US-Iran peace deal. Major global energy firms such as ExxonMobil and Valero saw share prices decline over 5%. While oil and gas supply normalization will take time, the market has largely priced in short-term supply improvements. South Korean energy and refining stocks are under increased short-term selling pressure, while renewables and airline sectors benefit from reduced fuel costs.

💡 Analyst Insights
The sharp oil price drop compresses refining, chemical, and power generation margins, leading to near-term earnings headwinds. South Korean firms SK Innovation and S-Oil face direct profit pressure from lower crude prices and should be considered for medium-term underweight positioning. Conversely, Korean airlines Korean Air and Jeju Air stand to improve profitability due to lower fuel expenses. International beneficiaries include United Airlines and shipping giant Maersk. Recommended investment actions include reducing exposure to refining stocks and increasing allocations to aviation and leisure sectors. Investors should monitor geopolitical developments; failure of Iran to comply with the agreement could drive a rebound in oil prices and increase regional risk premiums.
