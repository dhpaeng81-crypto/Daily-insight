Data Center Power Use Quadruples by 2035, Boosting Energy and Semiconductor Stocks

Surging AI and data center electricity demand drives strong gains in energy and IT infrastructure sectors

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

💡 Analyst Insights
The rapid increase in electricity demand from data centers and AI growth provides both short- and mid-term catalysts for power, energy, and IT infrastructure sectors. In Korea, LS Cable & System is positioned to benefit from expanded data center power supply contracts, while Korea Electric Power Corporation KPS (KEPCO KPS) is seeing growing orders for power asset maintenance and upgrades. Globally, Nvidia continues to expand sales of AI-driven data center solutions, reinforcing its sector leadership. We recommend accumulating IT infrastructure and energy stocks in the near term and increasing exposure to AI supply chain leaders over the medium term. Key risks include a global rebound in interest rates, tightening regulations, and slowing economic growth in China, which could trigger market corrections.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

Electricity demand from data centers is projected to quadruple by 2035, with new facilities opening by 2033 consuming as much power as the entire country of India today. Google recently unveiled new Gemini AI models, though the absence of the anticipated 3.5 Pro variant raises questions about its strategic direction. Meanwhile, OpenAI acknowledged responsibility for a security breach involving its Hugging Face pre-release models, highlighting emerging risks in AI data governance. These developments create broad growth opportunities across Korean telecom, IT services, server, and semiconductor sectors.

Energy Energy · Industry

Forecasts indicate data center electricity consumption will grow fourfold by 2035, lifting prices across power, LNG, and renewable energy sectors. Brent crude oil prices edged up 1.3% to $86 per barrel, and LNG spot prices rose about 4%. Despite recent governmental electricity price relief measures in the U.S. and Europe, underlying energy demand continues to climb. In South Korea, government initiatives to strengthen power supply and expand renewables positively influence investor sentiment in local energy equities.
