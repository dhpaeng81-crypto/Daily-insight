Finance Financial Markets

The U.S. equity markets closed mixed amid significant buying by Warren Buffett's successor Greg Abel and sector-specific fluctuations. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 0.7%, the Nasdaq Composite dropped 0.4%, and the S&P 500 edged up 0.2%. Financials and energy sectors led gains, rising 1.2% and 1.5%, respectively, while real estate declined 1.0% and IT sectors softened 0.3% amid global AI competition and uncertainty. The benchmark 10-year U.S. Treasury yield inched up to 4.3%, whereas the dollar index weakened by 0.4%.

💡 Analyst Insights
The financial and energy sectors appear poised for mid-term outperformance due to robust earnings growth and attractive dividends. In South Korea, S-Oil benefits directly from rising oil prices and substantial foreign capital inflow, while KB Financial Group gains from increasing interest income amid higher rates. On a global scale, asset manager Brookfield is actively reallocating funds into private infrastructure and energy companies, signaling growing investor interest in these segments. We recommend increasing exposure to South Korean refining stocks such as S-Oil and SK Innovation to capture margin expansion. However, investors should remain cautious of risks including potential Federal Reserve rate hikes, escalating US-China tensions, and possible oil price corrections.

Energy Energy · Industry

ExxonMobil executives have issued warnings of a sharp surge in international oil prices potentially reaching $160 per barrel, while crude benchmarks jumped intraday by over 3% (WTI at $92, Brent at $95). Geopolitical tensions in Europe and the Middle East, along with drone attacks in Ukraine, are driving short-term volatility. Natural gas prices rose 1.8%, affected by prolonged heatwaves in the U.S. East Coast and Europe. Meanwhile, renewable energy growth is temporarily subdued due to cost challenges and delays in power transmission infrastructure.

💡 Analyst Insights
The spike in crude oil prices offers South Korea’s refining, petrochemical, and shipping sectors attractive margin expansion and short-term earnings upsides. Companies like S-Oil and Hyundai Heavy Industries stand to benefit from wider crude spreads, increased ship order volumes, and expanding LNG businesses. Globally, ExxonMobil is directly advantaged by rising crude and LNG prices. We advise overweight positions in refining and shipping stocks to capitalize on this favorable environment. Investors should remain vigilant of risks including easing geopolitical tensions, which could precipitate sharp price declines, and demand uncertainty from China’s economic outlook.
