US Eases Iran Oil Sanctions; Korean Refiners and Steelmakers Rebound

Temporary lifting of Iran oil import ban stabilizes energy prices, boosting cyclical sectors

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

The KOSPI ended 0.6% higher at 2,793 points while the KOSDAQ rose 1.1% to 915 points, driven by optimism following the US's partial 60-day lifting of sanctions on Iranian crude oil imports. This eased energy price pressures, benefiting energy-sensitive sectors such as steel, refining, and airlines. Key Korean stocks like POSCO Holdings and Korean Air surged 2-4% as foreign hedge funds increased buying activity. Meanwhile, the US Nasdaq gained 0.8% despite Alphabet (Google) dropping 2.7%, supported by strength in AI semiconductor stocks and ongoing foreign capital inflows.

💡 Analyst Insights
The sanction relaxation on Iranian crude led to a short-term 5% drop in oil prices, directly benefiting sectors sensitive to energy costs including airlines, steel, and transport. Korean Air, which allocates about 60% of its cost to energy, and leading steelmaker POSCO Holdings saw respective share price rebounds of 2-4%. Globally, ExxonMobil is also poised for improved earnings linked to oil price variability. For investors, increasing exposure to Korean refining (e.g., SK Innovation, S-Oil), steel, and airline stocks is advisable in the near term (1-3 months). However, ongoing geopolitical risks in the Middle East and uncertainties around Federal Reserve interest rate policies warrant caution as they could reignite commodity volatility.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

Alphabet faced a 2.7% share drop following the departure of key AI leadership, highlighting intensifying competition for tech talent. Conversely, AI chipmaker Groq raised $650 million (around 8.8 trillion KRW), challenging market leader Nvidia, whose market capitalization surpassed 3,300 trillion KRW. Google DeepMind is investing $75 million to develop AI-driven video production tools, targeting the entertainment industry. The commercialization of 'agent swarm' AI technology is accelerating, positioning South Korea’s AI solution providers, data centers, and semiconductor manufacturers for significant gains.

💡 Analyst Insights
Domestically, SK Hynix leads with a 60% market share in AI server memory, positioning it as a primary beneficiary. Naver, through its subsidiary Naver Cloud, anticipates annual revenue growth exceeding 25% due to expanded AI engine deployments. Nvidia remains the top global beneficiary, especially in data center energy efficiency and GPU market expansion. Energy Energy · Industry

Following the US authorization of a temporary Iranian oil import waiver until August 21, international crude prices dropped approximately 2%, settling around $81 per barrel. China's crude inventories have exceeded 1 billion barrels, indicating a temporarily eased supply-demand balance. Canada announced plans to double its nuclear power grid capacity by 2050, signaling increased investment in nuclear-related industries. Globally, the energy sector is gradually shifting away from oil and natural gas towards nuclear and renewable energy sources, reflecting evolving market structures.

💡 Analyst Insights
The oil price decline enhances profit margins for South Korean refiners such as SK Innovation and S-Oil, as well as energy-intensive sectors including POSCO Holdings and Korean Air. Concurrently, expanded nuclear infrastructure investments favor companies like Doosan Enerbility and POSCO Future M, which focus on nuclear components and advanced materials. Canadian nuclear operator Cameco also stands to benefit from this strategic shift. A tactical allocation overweighting refining, steel, and airline stocks is recommended short term, while a medium-term buy position in the nuclear value chain companies is prudent. Key risks remain geopolitical instability in the Middle East and potential demand shocks from global economic slowdowns.
