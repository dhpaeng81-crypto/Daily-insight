Oil and AI Stocks Surge Amid Middle East Ceasefire and Data Center Power Demand

Geopolitical relief and AI infrastructure needs drive energy and tech sectors higher.

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

U.S. equities closed higher with consumer discretionary and tech sectors leading gains, as oil prices fell sharply by 2.5% amid Middle East ceasefire hopes. The S&P 500 tech sector rose 1.8%, and consumer discretionary increased 2.2%, supported by themes like robotaxis, travel, and gaming. However, retail stocks declined 1.1% due to a major grocery chain facing fines for price fixing allegations. The yield on the U.S. 10-year Treasury dropped 5 basis points to 4.22%, signaling increased risk appetite. The Korean won strengthened 0.4% to 1,375 per USD, benefiting from a softer dollar environment.

Energy Energy · Industry

International oil prices declined to the low $80s per barrel, down 2.5%, reflecting easing geopolitical tensions following a Middle East ceasefire framework and a partial normalization of supply chains. Despite drone attacks damaging Russian refining capacity, global inventory builds and demand concerns limited further price spikes. Renewables are gaining traction amid long-term regional energy shifts, particularly in Southeast Asia, as investment in solar and green power ramps up. U.S. regulatory moves to expedite power grid connections for data centers further intertwine energy and AI infrastructure sectors.
