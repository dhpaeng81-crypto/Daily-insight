Nasdaq Falls 1.8%; Geothermal Energy IPO Highlights Sector Divergence

Tech stocks stumble while new energy listings surge amid mixed growth and risk signals

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

The Nasdaq Composite declined 1.8%, marking its sixth consecutive session of correction driven by broad technology sector weakness. Notably, software company The Trade Desk plunged nearly 8%, exacerbating short-term market uncertainty. Cryptocurrency-related stocks also weakened selectively; CleanSpark dropped 12% following disappointing earnings. This dual movement between large-cap stability and small-cap volatility coincides with a 0.5% month-over-month decline in the U.S. Leading Economic Index for May, signaling a potential trend reversal.

Investors should adopt a cautious stance amid ongoing market adjustments. Large-cap tech firms like Samsung Electronics and Naver benefit from stable valuations and consistent fund inflows, making them less sensitive to short-term volatility. Energy Energy · Industry

U.S. geothermal energy startup Fervo successfully completed its IPO, spotlighting clean power generation via advanced drilling techniques adapted from oil and gas industries. The geothermal sector is expected to grow annually by over 10% through 2026, supported by expanding renewable energy supply chains in the U.S. and Europe. International crude oil prices have edged up modestly to around $78 per barrel in mid-June, reflecting gradual supply recovery in electricity and other energy resources. This environment creates favorable conditions for continued investments in renewable infrastructure.
