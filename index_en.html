Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices retraced by 1.3% amid restored Middle Eastern supply expectations but attempted a short-term recovery, with WTI crude stabilizing around $77 per barrel. The reopening of the Strait of Hormuz has not translated into immediate normalized flows, anticipated only by 2025. Complicating factors include drone attacks on Russian refinery infrastructure and political uncertainties within U.S. oil production. This backdrop maintains elevated volatility in energy markets and cloud short-term prospects for renewable energy investments.
