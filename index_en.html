AT&T gained from market share expansion after a rival's bankruptcy, while health insurers face premium hikes. Sector rotation is prominent, signaling differentiated industrial performances.

Insurance, telecommunications, and energy sectors are poised for short-term gains. In Korea, life insurer Samsung Life Insurance benefits from a rising premium trend, and telecom giant SK Telecom stands to gain from reduced competition. Globally, AT&T is likely to improve profitability due to near-term market dominance after a competitor’s exit. We recommend increasing allocations in Korean insurance (Samsung Life), telecom (SK Telecom), and energy sectors. However, investors should monitor macro risks such as potential economic slowdown triggered by energy price spikes, consumer spending reductions, and bond market instability.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI market is growing robustly at over 30% annually, driven by enterprise automation and data center investments. Meta recently launched 'Muse Spark 1.1,' enhancing AI coding automation and supporting large-scale enterprise workloads. Global corporations, including Starbucks, are diversifying AI vendors to optimize operations. While AI adoption expands, copyright challenges raise regulatory risks, emphasizing the importance of platform reliability and trust. Korean AI specialists and data center operators are positioned to benefit directly from these trends.

Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices rose to around $85 per barrel due to shipping delays through the Strait of Hormuz, while natural gas prices declined 2.4% amid increased U.S. storage and LNG export facility maintenance. Continued global supply uncertainties support strength in traditional energy stocks. Concurrently, new renewable energy investments and Canadian oil pipeline expansions were announced. The market shows temporary rotations into utilities and energy equipment stocks alongside energy producers.
