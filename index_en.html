US Employment Slowdown & Oil Price Drop Boost Stocks and Bonds

Weaker US jobs data lifts Fed rate cut hopes; oil falls 5%, IT sector gains

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

US nonfarm payrolls growth decelerated 25% in June, dropping from 200,000 to 150,000 new jobs, triggering a 1.8% decline in the US dollar. The S&P 500 rose 0.7%, supported by defensive sectors, IT firms, and energy price-sensitive industries like airlines and chemicals. The 10-year Treasury yield fell from 4.34% to 4.17%, reflecting easing monetary policy expectations. In Asia, South Korea's KOSPI index advanced 0.6%, while Japan’s Nikkei 225 gained 0.4%, as global capital rotated into growth and tech stocks.

💡 Analyst Insights
The Fed’s anticipated rate cuts are favoring IT and consumer staples sectors in the near term. South Korean market winners include Samsung Electronics, benefiting from surging demand for memory semiconductors and AI servers, and LG Household & Health Care, a defensive consumer staple boosted by the strong won. Globally, Apple stands out with sturdy iPhone 15 pre-orders cushioning from US labor market weakness. We recommend increasing portfolio weights in these areas, supported by a 70% probability of a 0.25 percentage point Fed rate cut in September from 5.25%. Caution is warranted if employment data deteriorates further, possibly dampening earnings and triggering profit-taking.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

Despite some deceleration in AI agent development among global giants like Meta and Microsoft, the AI/IT sector remains robust due to soaring data center power demands and intensified browser competition. South Korea’s AI market is forecasted to hit 15 trillion won (~$11.3 billion) in 2024, growing annually at 26%. Leading domestic players such as Naver and Kakao are expanding AI-based services and investing heavily in data center infrastructure. Heightened competition in browser, search, and cloud subsectors from emerging startups is also reshaping the landscape.

💡 Analyst Insights
Data centers, cloud infrastructure, and AI semiconductors are short-term focus areas with strong growth potential. Korean beneficiaries include SK Hynix, whose AI DRAM products are expected to make up 42% of AI server revenue in 2024, and Naver, which is enhancing earnings through flagship AI and cloud services. Globally, NVIDIA remains dominant with an 85% share in AI semiconductor chips. We advise a buy stance on these sectors, using phased entries during market pullbacks in H1. Key risks include AI frothiness, regulatory challenges (privacy laws, power shortages), and intensifying competition among major IT firms.
Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices plunged about 5% to $75 per barrel (WTI) on resumed exports from Middle Eastern producers like Saudi Arabia and Iran, coupled with weaker US job growth signaling demand slowing in H2. Meanwhile, US electricity prices are climbing sharply, up 25%, pressured by surging data center power consumption. Faster-than-expected oil supply recovery has spotlighted energy efficiency and renewable investments. Globally, government policies are increasingly prioritizing clean energy and demand management.

💡 Analyst Insights
The 5% drop in crude oil prices improves cost competitiveness for chemical, airline, and transport companies. Korean firms such as Korean Air benefit through reduced jet fuel expenses, supporting an earnings rebound, while Lotte Chemical sees margin improvements from cheaper feedstock like ethylene. On the international front, NextEra Energy gains from ongoing US renewable power and grid infrastructure investments. Our recommendation is to tactically overweight energy-linked stocks short term, while monitoring geopolitical risks like Middle East tensions or sudden demand shocks. Quick response to policy shifts on power pricing and renewables is critical.
