Apple, OpenAI IPOs Nearing, Renewed Focus on AI-Related Stocks

AI IPO momentum and Apple's new features fuel buying in IT and secondary battery sectors

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

The US technology sector faced a sharp correction with the Direxion 3x Tech ETF (TECL) plunging 19.9%, and the Nasdaq technology-focused XLK ETF declining 6.7%. Conversely, energy and consumer staples sectors outperformed amid a 3.2% rise in crude oil prices driven by escalating Iran-Israel geopolitical tensions and resilient US retail demand. Expectations of a Federal Reserve rate hold in the second half of the year stabilized Treasury yields near 4.3%, reinforcing demand for safe assets. Korea's benchmark KOSPI gained 0.8%, led by secondary battery and IT stocks, supported by net foreign buying of approximately 150 billion KRW.

💡 Analyst Insights
In the short term, buying momentum is expected to concentrate on IT and secondary battery sectors. Samsung Electronics and LG Energy Solution stand out, benefiting from Apple's AI-driven new business initiatives and the growing global demand for advanced batteries, indicating substantial further upside potential. Nvidia, the global AI semiconductor leader, is also poised for a rebound of around 6% fueled by OpenAI and Apple-related IPO optimism. The recommended investment stance is to increase portfolio weightings ('overweight') in these sectors. However, investors should remain cautious of downside risks stemming from rising interest rates, oil prices, and geopolitical uncertainties, which could trigger sharp sell-offs.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The global AI industry is accelerating capital inflows as both OpenAI and Anthropic have filed confidentially for IPOs, signaling a fierce competitive race. Apple unveiled AI-integrated features at WWDC, including photo enhancement and predictive text completion, rapidly expanding its iPhone-centered ecosystem. In Korea, AI-related hardware demand is expected to grow for companies such as Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix, while Naver Cloud is positioned to benefit on the solutions side. The worldwide generative AI market is forecasted to reach 70 trillion KRW in 2024, representing a 28% year-on-year increase.

Energy Energy · Industry

Tensions in the Middle East, marked by the 100-day milestone of missile exchanges between Iran and Israel, have driven oil prices higher by 3.2%. Credit rating agencies like Fitch have revised their outlooks, projecting sustained price increases through 2025. US approvals for oil expansion projects (including Quantico 1) promise accelerated production growth. Meanwhile, US clean energy policy uncertainty has eased following a favorable court ruling on renewable energy tax credits. Electricity and gas markets are forecasted to tighten through 2026, suggesting an additional 10% rise in energy prices this year.
