Oil Rises Over 1%, AI Power Demand Soars: Energy Stocks in Focus

Surging AI-driven power needs and geopolitical risks boost energy sector volatility.

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global financial markets experienced mixed trends with technology stocks retreating while energy shares surged. The S&P 500 declined by 0.5%, and the Nasdaq Composite fell 1.1%, reflecting pressure on tech-heavy stocks. Conversely, crude oil prices climbed above $79 per barrel, driven by geopolitical tensions, lifting the energy sector by 1.4%. Defense stocks also gained 0.8%, whereas semiconductor and cloud computing segments fell by 1.7% and 2.6%, respectively. U.S. Treasury yields remained relatively stable with the 2-year note at 4.70% and the 10-year at 4.27%, while the dollar maintained its strength in currency markets.

💡 Analyst Insights
The energy sector currently benefits from heightened geopolitical risks, including drone attacks affecting supply security, alongside rising electricity demand driven by AI advancement. In South Korea, Korea Electric Power Corporation (KEPCO) stands out due to increased domestic power consumption and supportive policies, while SK Innovation profits from higher crude prices and improved refining margins. Globally, ExxonMobil remains a key beneficiary of the energy price rally. An effective investment approach involves increasing exposure to energy stocks and selectively allocating funds to companies involved in power infrastructure and smart grid technology, given AI-powered demand growth. However, investors should remain cautious of potential risks such as easing geopolitical tensions that could trigger sharp energy price corrections and a rebound in tech shares. A strategy emphasizing phased buying and diversification is recommended to manage volatility.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and IT sectors are entering a phase of AI investment consolidation, where earnings-driven stock selection becomes critical. Apple announced upgrades to Siri focusing on enhanced privacy features set to roll out next year. Semiconductor companies like Nvidia and AMD remain well-positioned thanks to rising data center power demands fueled by AI workloads. The global AI market is projected to grow at a compound annual rate of 37%, reaching an estimated KRW 432 trillion by 2027, creating cross-sector opportunities for Korean semiconductor, data center, and power companies. Nevertheless, some high-growth stocks like Wix.com have experienced sharp declines of up to 30% due to underwhelming expectations, underscoring the importance of performance-based investment.

Energy Energy · Industry

International oil prices rebounded by over 1% following a drone attack on a nuclear facility in the United Arab Emirates, pushing Brent crude above $79 per barrel. The widespread adoption of AI technologies is sharply increasing power consumption, driving higher utilization rates across traditional energy sources such as natural gas, coal, and nuclear plants. In the U.S., proposed mega-mergers like the $400 billion deal between NextEra Energy and Dominion Energy signal ongoing consolidation in the energy sector. Additionally, energy security is being strengthened with landmark deals like the India-UAE long-term oil storage agreement, enhancing supply resilience.
