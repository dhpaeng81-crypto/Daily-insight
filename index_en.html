💡 Analyst Insights
Capital concentration in semiconductors and eco-friendly growth stocks warrants a tactical overweight in these sectors. Leading Korean beneficiaries include SK hynix, with its massive overseas US IPO bolstering its AI semiconductor capacity, and Samsung Electronics, backed by recovering memory and foundry operations. On the global front, Nvidia continues to capitalize on expanding cloud and AI chip demand. Investment strategy suggests a tactical buy over the next 3 to 6 months to leverage earnings momentum and inflows, but investors should monitor risks from delayed US rate cuts and escalating US-China semiconductor export restrictions that could trigger volatility.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

SK hynix successfully raised $26.5 billion in the largest US IPO by a foreign company, underscoring the boom in AI semiconductor manufacturing. Major tech companies such as OpenAI and Apple are intensifying competition in AI and security technologies. The proliferation of open-source AI and intensified demand for AI data centers introduces new operational challenges, including resource competition like agricultural water usage. Globally, the AI data center market is expected to grow at a 22% CAGR, reaching $400 billion by 2027, signaling sizable expansion and investment opportunities for Korea’s IT hardware and software sectors.

AI semiconductor components, including memory and high-performance accelerators, are key near-term growth drivers. SK hynix’s orders for GDDR and HBM memory have surged, while Samsung Electronics benefits from recovery in foundry and AI memory sectors. Nvidia remains the dominant player globally in AI chips. Energy Energy · Industry

Geopolitical tensions, particularly involving Iran, pushed Brent crude prices up 1.9% to $86.20 per barrel, fueling short-term strength in energy stocks. Volatility in gas and electricity markets has increased due to Middle East conflicts and disruptions in Australian LNG supply, yet energy companies are reporting improved earnings. Renewable energy sectors benefit from surging global data center demand, improving supply-demand balance. Korean refiners and power generation companies stand to gain amid these dynamics, supported by an uptick in oil and gas prices internationally.
