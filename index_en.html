AMD Surges 303% on AI Demand; Middle East Energy Risks Intensify

AI-driven semiconductor gains and energy supply concerns dominate market sentiment.

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

US technology-heavy indices continued their upward momentum, led by AMD's remarkable 303% year-over-year stock surge fueled by AI demand. In contrast, consumer discretionary sectors, notably chain restaurants and health clubs, underperformed, with Planet Fitness experiencing a sharp decline due to falling new memberships. Long-term investor interest increasingly converges on tech and energy sectors. Meanwhile, the 10-year US Treasury yield edged down to about 4.3%, supporting a revived risk-on appetite in global markets.

💡 Analyst Insights
Semiconductors and data infrastructure linked to AI remain the critical sectors for portfolio overweighting both short and medium term. Korean tech giants Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix are prime beneficiaries; their AI and high-performance memory shipments have each grown over 30% annually, positioning them well for sustained earnings growth. Globally, AMD's stellar share price appreciation underscores robust demand from expanding data center investments. Our tactical recommendation favors increasing exposure to technology stocks while reducing allocations to low-growth consumer staples and discretionary sectors. Investors should monitor US consumer spending patterns and Federal Reserve policy for potential headwinds that could reverse positive momentum.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

Rapid expansion of AI applications and infrastructure is a defining trend in 2024, with large-scale domestic and international data center investments pushing the global market size beyond 200 trillion KRW (approx. $160 billion). The Korean government, alongside private sector initiatives, is aggressively enhancing AI and cloud infrastructure capacity. Concurrently, demand for AI-driven cybersecurity, automation, and predictive analytics—key components of smart factory initiatives—is accelerating, reflecting broader digital transformation across industries.

💡 Analyst Insights
AI semiconductors, memory chips, and data center-related companies are prominently positioned for growth in H1 2024. SK Hynix stands out as the world’s top shipper of high-bandwidth memory (HBM), while Naver is scaling its AI-powered data center capacity aiming to double by year-end, indicating strong growth momentum. On a global scale, Microsoft's cloud-AI integrated services lead this booming market, with projected revenue growth of 27% in 2024. Investment strategy emphasizes overweighting these AI and data infrastructure themes. Nonetheless, emerging risks include regulatory developments on AI usage and supply bottlenecks in critical hardware like servers and rocket launches for space-based data centers, necessitating diversified portfolio entries to mitigate medium-term volatility.
Energy Energy · Industry

Energy markets have reacted sharply to escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, notably near the Strait of Hormuz, driving Brent crude prices up 7% week-over-week to $86 per barrel. Saudi Aramco warned that a blockade could disrupt supply by as much as 100 million barrels weekly, intensifying global supply concerns. Asian nations are budgeting around $70 per barrel but remain alert to further shocks. Iran’s Revolutionary Guards continue exerting pressure on oil trading, while North American producers expand domestic energy production to offset potential shortages. Renewable energy and battery storage investments are also gaining momentum as strategic hedges against volatility.

💡 Analyst Insights
The spike in energy prices favors upstream sectors such as raw materials, shipbuilding, refining, and power generation, whereas industries like airlines, transportation, and chemicals face immediate cost pressures. Among Korean firms, SK Innovation benefits from improving refining margins, and Korean Shipbuilding & Offshore Engineering is capitalizing on increased energy transport vessel orders. Globally, Canada’s Cenovus Energy projects near doubling of net profits, supported by acquisitions and a high-price environment. Investment actions advise overweight positions in refining and shipbuilding with cautious, phased accumulation; conversely, overweighting in airlines and chemicals is discouraged. Given the risk of broader Middle East conflict escalation or a sharp global demand contraction due to recession fears, staged buying is prudent for energy equities.
