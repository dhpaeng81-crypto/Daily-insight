US-Iran nuclear talks trigger 7% oil plunge; mixed tech and energy stock reactions follow.

Major US equity indices showed mixed performances as consumer sentiment weakened and retail earnings disappointed. The S&P 500 declined 0.4%, the Dow Jones fell 0.6%, while the Nasdaq rose slightly by 0.2%, led by select IT gains. Volatility widened across sectors amid growing recession concerns and collapsing commodities prices.

Energy Energy · Industry

International crude oil prices plunged over 7% to near $98 per barrel amid renewed optimism on US-Iran nuclear negotiations. This reflects easing global supply concerns and increased US crude exports. Key oil importers such as Japan and Middle Eastern countries remain cautious amid ongoing supply disruptions, while renewable energy investments and energy diversification efforts are rising. Broad commodity indices also declined, triggering heightened volatility in Korean energy and petrochemical sectors.

💡 Analyst Insights
The sharp oil price decline lowers costs for power and industrial sectors, benefiting manufacturing and transportation companies, while pressuring refining and oil producers’ profitability. In Korea, Korean Air likely gains from reduced jet fuel costs, and Samsung Electronics benefits indirectly via lower manufacturing expenses aiding semiconductor exports. Conversely, refiners like S-Oil and GS Caltex face margin contractions and near-term earnings risk. Internationally, ExxonMobil pursues investment expansion alongside profitability defenses. Current tactical advice is to reduce energy stock holdings and increase exposure to export-oriented manufacturers, while remaining alert to potential rebounds from geopolitical flare-ups or US oil production disruptions.
