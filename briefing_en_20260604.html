Nvidia and Google Drive AI Surge; IT and Energy Attract Capital

Strong AI and energy sector momentum shifts capital toward large-cap IT and energy stocks

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

The KOSPI rose 0.8% to 2,805 points and KOSDAQ gained 1.2% to 924 points, led by IT and AI-related stocks. In the US, the S&P 500 advanced 0.4%, while the Nasdaq edged up 0.7%, buoyed by Nvidia’s strong AI performance. European markets showed mixed results amid Eurozone uncertainty, particularly in financial sectors. Global bond yields softened slightly, with the US 10-year Treasury yield falling to 4.37%. Emerging markets are seeing revived inflows into IT and energy sectors, underscoring a global preference shift toward these areas.

💡 Analyst Insights
AI semiconductors, major IT stocks, and energy sectors are clear short-term beneficiaries. South Korea’s Samsung Electronics is poised to gain from expanding AI server demand and semiconductor growth, while SK Hynix leads globally in AI DRAM production. On the global front, Nvidia's stock surged 8% following its new AI PC launch, with Dell’s shares increasing 4.5% as a key supply partner. The recommended strategy is to increase portfolio weightings in large-cap IT and energy sectors (refiners, power generators). However, investors should remain cautious of potential AI theme overheating, the risk of renewed US rate hikes, and geopolitical tensions in Ukraine and the Middle East that could increase market volatility in the near term.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

Expansion of AI and cloud-based services is accelerating globally. Google has raised $85 billion via new stock issuance to fund aggressive AI R&D, pioneering innovations such as 'Dreambeans' and 'Anthropic Claude.' Lovable, a key enterprise client, signed a 5-year deal increasing their Google Cloud usage fivefold, signaling robust growth in enterprise AI adoption. Korean cloud and AI infrastructure demand is expected to surge midterm, enhancing outlooks for IT service providers like Samsung SDS and NAVER Cloud.

Energy Energy · Industry

Brent crude oil rebounded 0.7% to $83.1 per barrel amid ongoing uncertainty around Middle East ceasefire prospects, maintaining short-term price volatility. US natural gas prices declined 7.2% over the past two weeks to $2.55 per million BTU due to rising inventories. Renewables, including solar and battery sectors, experienced improved investor sentiment backed by government support. While European power prices dipped slightly reflecting economic slowdown, underlying supply risks from Iran and Russia sustain a generally firm energy market posture.
