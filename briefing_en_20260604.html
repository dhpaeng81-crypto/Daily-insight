Alphabet Raises $85B for AI, Driving Surge in Korean IT and Energy Stocks

AI investment heat intensifies; energy sector faces short-term supply tensions

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Over the past week, U.S. markets showed modest fluctuations with the Nasdaq Composite edging up 0.7%, while the S&P 500 declined 0.3%. The financial sector underperformed, down 1.2%, pressured by concerns over potential Federal Reserve interest rate hikes following hawkish comments from Fed officials. Retail continues structural adjustments highlighted by a major global fashion retailer’s store closures. Meanwhile, technology stocks, notably AI-related names, rallied strongly, fueled by Alphabet’s record $85 billion capital raise. Benchmark U.S. 10-year Treasury yields rose by 5 basis points to 4.43%.

💡 Analyst Insights
In the near term, AI and semiconductor sectors are experiencing an influx of active buying, with room for further gains over the next three months. Korean leaders such as Samsung Electronics stand to benefit from both semiconductor demand and augmented AI investments worldwide. NHN is also poised for profitability improvement due to expanded AI-driven cloud platform services. On the global front, Nvidia remains a key beneficiary of AI advancement, with upside potential exceeding 10% in the short term. We recommend increasing portfolio weights in these sectors while closely monitoring the Federal Reserve's potential 25 basis point rate hikes, which may increase preference for cash holdings. Geopolitical tensions, particularly in the Middle East, also remain downside risks.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

Alphabet’s unprecedented $85 billion funding round for Google’s AI operations highlights the accelerating global AI investment momentum. South Korean IT conglomerates are intensifying efforts in AI platforms, data services, and security solutions, including AI-based authentication. International tech giants like Google and Amazon are rapidly introducing AI-enhanced products that improve user experience and bolster data protection frameworks. The global AI market is projected to grow annually by over 15%, reaching approximately 600 trillion KRW by 2027. This expansion is resulting in noticeable sales improvements across Korea's semiconductor, cloud computing, data analytics, and cybersecurity sectors.

Korean semiconductor companies such as Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix are expected to maintain strong buying interest in response to rising AI demand impacting memory and non-memory segments. NHN and Naver are capturing cloud AI platform growth domestically. Globally, Alphabet and Amazon continue expanding their AI cloud services and customized AI product offerings, increasing market share. Energy Energy · Industry

International crude oil prices surged about 4%, reaching $81 per barrel amid U.S. crude export restrictions and escalating tensions related to Iran in the Middle East. Conversely, natural gas prices declined around 3%, influenced by increased inventory levels in the U.S. Concurrently, major electricity providers in the U.S. are proposing 5-7% increases in electricity rates. Heightened geopolitical conflicts between the U.S. and Iran, alongside the reevaluation of energy codes in Europe, have amplified market uncertainty. Although renewable energy policies are gaining ground in the U.S. and Europe, short-term supply concerns continue to pressure fossil fuel prices upward.
