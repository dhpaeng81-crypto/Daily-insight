Expanding Domestic AI, Energy, and Bond Opportunities amid Market Volatility

Rate cuts anticipated, AI and energy segments gain; CRM and NICE up 10%, oil down 8%, bond demand rises

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

U.S. markets saw increased bond buying amid hopes for rate cuts and heightened stock volatility. Over the past three days, Nasdaq dropped 2.2%, S&P 500 declined 1.8%, while the 10-year U.S. Treasury yield fell by 0.17 percentage points to 4.25%. Financial stocks tied to fixed income and cash assets gained, alongside AI beneficiaries such as NICE and cloud leader Salesforce, which surged nearly 10%. Foreign investors are reallocating towards stable dividend payers and fixed-income products, as energy sector volatility increases.

Renewables are gaining traction, illustrated by wave energy projects in Canada’s Great Lakes and new nuclear power facilities in the U.S. and Europe. Efforts to diversify supply chains expand growth opportunities for Korea’s refining and renewable power sectors.
