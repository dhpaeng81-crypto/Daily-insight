Energy cost pressures and AI-driven growth stocks gain renewed investor focus globally.

💡 Analyst Insights
The growth-oriented technology sector, especially AI and semiconductors, remains poised for short-term gains. Korean heavyweights Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix benefit from improving global semiconductor supply-demand dynamics and expanding orders from North American tech giants, underpinning solid earnings momentum. Internationally, ASML’s advanced lithography equipment continues to be a key beneficiary in semiconductor capital expenditure. We recommend increasing portfolio weightings in tech and semiconductors, with an emphasis on Korean equities, which have gained over 15% this year. Investors should remain alert to risks from potential U.S. interest rate hikes, earnings volatility in large-cap U.S. tech stocks, and geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and Eastern Europe.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

AI-powered video creation, gaming, and data automation technologies are rapidly reaching commercial maturity. Google expanded its AI Vids platform with personalized avatars and seamless app integration, while Roblox introduced AI-assisted game development features accessible via simple text prompts. Korea’s cloud computing, content creation, and semiconductor memory sectors (notably server DRAM and HBM) are entering a growth phase starting late Q2. The domestic AI market is projected to grow at a 17% annual rate, with the Korean AI semiconductor market expected to surpass KRW 3 trillion in 2024.

Energy Energy · Industry

Energy markets have seen a resurgence with West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude prices climbing 3.7% over the past week to around $81 per barrel, propelled by a decline in oil tanker movements through the Strait of Hormuz and policy uncertainties in renewables. Natural gas prices in Europe and the U.S. have rebounded between 4% and 7% over the last month. In South Korea, rising fuel import costs pressure utilities like Korea Electric Power Corporation (KEPCO), likely squeezing second-half earnings. Conversely, policy support is strengthening for wind and solar power producers domestically and abroad, amid global regulatory shifts in key energy markets such as the UK and Canada.
