Finance Financial Markets

US major financial indexes closed up 1.2%, led by large banks like JPMorgan initiating aggressive share buybacks and dividend hikes. Energy and materials sectors gained over 2%, fueled by concerns over Russian-Ukrainian supply disruptions. Technology stocks traded mixed with marginal 0.3% swings reflecting profit-taking. The 10-year US Treasury yield eased slightly to 4.30%, boosting hopes for potential interest rate cuts. Foreign capital inflows favored Korean banks, steel, and energy sectors (+450 billion KRW net), while semiconductors and secondary battery stocks saw cautious investor sentiment.

💡 Analyst Insights
The financial sector's strength today primarily stems from direct catalysts such as expanded dividends and substantial share repurchases, enhancing short-term momentum. Korean banks KB Financial Group and Shinhan Financial Group adopted similar aggressive buyback plans this year with KRW 1.2 trillion and KRW 0.8 trillion earmarked, offering attractive dividend yields of approximately 5.8% and 5.5%, respectively. Globally, JPMorgan’s $65 billion (approx. KRW 88 trillion) buyback plan underscores its confidence but valuation near its five-year average P/E of 11x signals limited upside. Our intermediate outlook remains positive with a BUY stance for financials, albeit investors should monitor US election uncertainty and potential tightening regulatory measures on buybacks and earnings. We advise focusing on dividend and buyback plays while maintaining cautious hedges against global policy shifts through H2 2024.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI sector is experiencing a recalibration phase where expansive investment meets regulatory scrutiny. French startup Mistral AI raised about KRW 700 billion within a year, intensifying competition with OpenAI and signaling acceleration in frontier AI development. Alibaba's recent ban on using the advanced Claude Code highlights rising Chinese concerns over AI reliability and data security. Nvidia unveiled its first 100% liquid-cooled AI data center, marking a breakthrough in AI infrastructure technology. However, the commercialization of AI by major players like Meta remains slower than anticipated, tempering near-term enthusiasm.

Energy Energy · Industry

Energy prices surged sharply following attacks on Russian oil and gas infrastructure, with Brent crude climbing 3.1% to surpass $88 per barrel. Ukrainian drone strikes on key pipelines and ports around St. Petersburg exacerbated supply concerns, pushing European natural gas prices up 4%. Concurrently, new pipeline projects in North America have reignited local opposition amid environmental and energy diversity debates. Renewable energy investments and distributed power generation capacity are expected to expand gradually as the energy transition continues.
