Warren Buffett Sells Entire Stake in High-Dividend Stocks Amid Market Rotation

Financials weaken early; energy and AI sectors attract short-term buying interest

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

Global and Korean financial markets displayed volatile mixed trading today. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rebounded 0.5% to reclaim the 38,000 level, supported by rising oil prices and shifting US Iran policies. However, the Korean financial sector lagged as high-dividend stocks faced sell-offs: Samsung Life dipped 1.2% and KB Financial fell 0.8%. Japanese yen depreciated 1% versus the dollar amid Bank of Japan’s indications of tapering bond purchases slower than expected, fueling bond yield uncertainties. Meanwhile, Nasdaq gained 1.3%, buoyed by semiconductor and AI optimism with Nvidia up 2.4% and Tesla 1.9%.

Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and data center market is on an upward trajectory due to massive investment inflows and intensifying technological competition. SoftBank announced a €75 billion (approx. KRW 100 trillion) plan to develop up to 5 gigawatts of additional data center capacity in France. Meta is advancing AI-based hardware innovations including wearable pendants. Korea's domestic AI service market surpassed KRW 5 trillion (+25% year-on-year) in 2023, while system semiconductor and server equipment exports surged 31% and 28% respectively in Q2 compared to last year. Concurrently, rising data center power demand is prompting heightened environmental concerns.

Energy Energy · Industry

Global crude oil prices rose 2.5% to USD 84.2 per barrel as Ukrainian forces struck Russia’s Saratov refinery, heightening supply concerns. In Europe, relaxed corporate car taxes have led to 59% of new vehicle registrations being internal combustion engines, temporarily boosting oil demand. Contrastingly, in the US, California’s renewable energy push drove wholesale electricity prices to record lows at 2.8 cents per kWh. The surge in AI data center energy consumption also brings new dynamics to power demand and renewables development.
