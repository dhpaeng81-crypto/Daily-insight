Iran's Strait of Hormuz Closure Sparks Oil Surge Above $92

Geopolitical tensions lift energy and defense stocks; growth stocks face profit-taking pressure

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

South Korea’s KOSPI fell 1.2% to hover in the low 2,600 range, reflecting increased foreign selling in major growth stocks. Cathie Wood notably exited nearly $60 million of growth stocks, amplifying profit-taking sentiment. US Dow Jones industrial futures also declined 0.8%, pressured by uncertainty around geopolitical risks. Meanwhile, energy and defense sectors rallied on Iran’s closure of the Strait of Hormuz and heightened Ukraine-related tensions. Safe-haven demand rose as US interest rate hike prospects receded, even though the US dollar showed short-term strength.

💡 Analyst Insights
Energy, defense, and raw materials sectors stand to benefit directly from escalating geopolitical tensions. Korean firms Samsung Engineering and Hanwha Aerospace have gained renewed investor interest due to expected Middle East plant projects and increased defense exports. Globally, ExxonMobil is a primary beneficiary from rising oil prices linked to Hormuz supply concerns. We recommend increasing exposure to energy and defense stocks in the near term while adopting a cautious stance on growth equities after their recent gains. Risks include a potential easing of tensions, triggering a pullback in energy and defense shares, and renewed volatility in US interest rates that may pressure global equity markets.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI sector faces intensifying competition amid controversies such as the US ban on Anthropic and talent departures from Google DeepMind. These developments highlight challenges in AI talent retention and regulatory scrutiny. Meanwhile, the surging demand for AI has significant implications for energy consumption, with rapid expansion in data centers driving power demand. South Korea’s AI market is projected to grow over 30% in 2024, surpassing 7 trillion won (approximately $5.4 billion). This growth is focusing investor attention on companies vital for AI data center infrastructure and power equipment.

Energy Energy · Industry

Iran's announcement of closing the Strait of Hormuz triggered an immediate oil price surge exceeding 8%, pushing Brent crude above $92 per barrel. Concurrently, Ukrainian drone strikes inflicted damage on Russian oil and gas facilities in Crimea and Siberia, exacerbating supply concerns. Declining US and European strategic petroleum reserves highlight tightening global supply fundamentals. While renewable energy investments such as solar continue to grow, especially tied to data center electricity needs, individual renewable stocks remain subdued. Korean oil refining and gas utility companies saw simultaneous stock gains amid revived fears of power shortages.
