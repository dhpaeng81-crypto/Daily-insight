AI Semiconductor Surge Boosts Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix

US AI demand drives strength in semiconductor and energy sectors; strategic overweight advised

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

The KOSPI rose 0.7% to close above 2,820, while the KOSDAQ gained 1.1%, driven by buying interest in small and mid-cap growth stocks. Positive market sentiment was fueled by the Federal Reserve signaling a pause on interest rate hikes, leading to increased global risk appetite. The technology, secondary battery, and semiconductor sectors outperformed with gains of 1.8%, 2.5%, and 2.2% respectively. The US Nasdaq index advanced 1.4%, led by a rally in semiconductor and AI-related shares. The USD-KRW exchange rate briefly dipped to the 1,385 level, supporting foreign net buying.

💡 Analyst Insights
Growth sectors centered on AI and semiconductors are leading the market rally. South Korean beneficiaries include Samsung Electronics, whose AI server DRAM shipments rose 40% in Q2, and LG Energy Solution, which secured a backlog exceeding 20 trillion KRW from overseas electric vehicle OEMs in the first half. Globally, Nvidia is a standout with 2024 revenue hitting 41.4 trillion KRW, a 300% increase year-over-year, attracting substantial capital inflows. Investors should consider overweighting Samsung Electronics and LG Energy Solution while also gaining exposure through ETFs such as the KODEX Semiconductor and TIGER Secondary Battery. Key risks remain in semiconductor oversupply and potential unexpected Fed rate hikes.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

AI and semiconductor markets are synergistically accelerating, highlighted by US AI chipmakers posting record annual revenue of 41.4 trillion KRW and net profits of 28.2 trillion KRW. This strong performance is boosting memory companies in Korea like Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix, who are expanding high-bandwidth DRAM shipments for AI servers and leading the global market share with HBM memory in AI training. The AI-driven demand surge is also broadening recruitment beyond traditional STEM fields to include humanities graduates. Despite operational challenges such as AI budget overruns, the overall market is expanding by over 30% compared to last year.

💡 Analyst Insights
The proliferation of AI semiconductors and data infrastructure is delivering short-term benefits to semiconductor, IT services, and related employment sectors. Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix are direct beneficiaries through ramped-up shipments of high-bandwidth memory products tailored for AI workloads. Globally, Nvidia commands the ecosystem as the world’s second-largest market cap company driven by explosive growth. An investment strategy emphasizing accumulation of large-cap AI and semiconductor stocks, along with relevant ETFs, is recommended through Q3. However, investors should be cautious of risks including tightening budgets for AI projects, increased regulatory scrutiny in the US and Europe, and the migration of key AI talent overseas.
Energy Energy · Industry

International crude oil prices edged up, with Brent crude reaching $83.2 per barrel (+1.5%), while US natural gas prices increased by 2.5%. Expanding renewable energy investment is exemplified by the UK attracting over £100 billion in private sector commitments, alongside the US Senate passing new energy legislation to advance clean infrastructure. Innovations in oil refining technology promise improved energy efficiency by 31.6%, cooling water savings of 20.7%, and CO2 emission reductions of 37.6%, pressuring Korean power and refining companies to adopt such advancements. Meanwhile, localized power supply disruptions in parts of the US are impacting industrial operations.

💡 Analyst Insights
The rise in oil and gas prices benefits power generation, refining, and green energy sectors. Key Korean names include Korea Electric Power Corporation (KEPCO), which is reducing operating losses via fuel cost-pass-through electricity tariff hikes, and Hyundai Energy Solutions, which surpassed 150 billion KRW in new solar project orders in the first half, domestically and abroad. National Grid in the UK also stands out as a beneficiary of clean energy investment and grid expansion. Overweighting renewable energy and efficiency equipment firms is advised in the near term, whereas smaller refining and power firms face margin pressure from rising input costs. Notable risks include sharp oil price declines, sudden drops in global demand triggered by US-China tensions, and withdrawal of supportive government policies.
