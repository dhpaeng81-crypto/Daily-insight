Nvidia Targets $266 Billion AI CPU Market, Tech and Energy Sectors Shift

Tech-driven capital rotation continues amid AI, renewable energy, and mixed sector performance

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

The US S&P 500 climbed 0.7%, led by a 0.9% gain in the Nasdaq, reflecting strong technology sector momentum. South Korea’s KOSPI mirrored this trend with Samsung Electronics gaining 2.4% and SK Hynix up 1.8%, driven by robust IT sector performance. Global bond yields declined about 0.08 percentage points across maturities following Federal Reserve comments, boosting risk appetite. China’s green industrial expansion fueled net purchases in renewables and secondary battery stocks, while falling oil prices pressured energy shares. The Korean won strengthened to the low 1,370 range against a weakening US dollar due to easing rate hike expectations.

Target has reported an unexpected shift in customer behavior, with consumers increasingly favoring essentials and value-oriented products over discretionary spending. The news triggered a modest decline in Target's stock as investors reassess growth prospects amid evolving consumer trends. China Turns Green Tech Into an Economic Lifeline
China is increasingly leveraging green technology as a critical driver for economic growth amid global uncertainties and domestic challenges. The country is investing heavily in renewable energy sectors such as solar, wind, and electric vehicles, positioning itself as a leader in sustainable innovation. This strategic focus is boosting related industries and attracting both domestic and foreign investments, helping to stabilize China's economy. Investors should watch policy developments, technology advancements, and international trade dynamics that could influence the growth trajectory of China’s green tech sector.

💡 Analyst Insights
Key beneficiary sectors are IT and renewable energy. Samsung SDI and LG Energy Solution are poised to gain 4.1% and 3.6% respectively, supported by China’s acceleration of clean technology investment. Foreign capital is strongly entering AI-related stocks such as Nvidia, while Tesla’s expanding renewable energy-linked battery business draws attention globally. We recommend overweighting AI and renewable energy themes with focused buys on Hanwha Solutions and LG Energy Solution domestically, alongside Nvidia and Tesla internationally. Risks include geopolitical volatility stemming from former President Trump’s statements on Middle East tensions, possible US-Iran conflict escalation, and intensified US-China trade frictions.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

Nvidia has forecasted the emergence of a $266 billion (approximately 266 trillion KRW) new AI CPU market focused on AI agents, signaling a significant industry shift. The company reported an 18% year-over-year revenue increase, reaching KRW 44 trillion, complemented by $58 billion invested in AI startups. AI company Anthropic anticipates its first profitable quarter with Q2 sales reaching $10.9 billion. Google introduced its Gemini 3.5 Flash and Omni AI models, improving processing speed and lowering costs, reshaping competitive dynamics. South Korean firms like SK Telecom and Naver are rapidly expanding AI services and strengthening foundational technologies, marking an intensifying domestic innovation race.

💡 Analyst Insights
AI market growth is concentrated in semiconductors, cloud infrastructure, and AI solutions. Samsung Electronics (notably in memory and cloud) and Naver (developing proprietary AI models and cloud services) are well-positioned beneficiaries with strong short-term upside. Nvidia’s entry into AI CPUs further supports medium-term gains for Samsung and SK Hynix as system semiconductor and solution providers. Globally, Nvidia and Google dominate AI infrastructure and platform innovation. We advise increasing portfolio weightings in leading Korean AI stocks and incremental buys of global AI tech giants. Investors should remain vigilant for short-term volatility risks from AI sector overheating, stricter regulations, and technology execution challenges.
Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices declined approximately 6%, adjusting to near $75 per barrel amid progressing US-Iran negotiations easing supply concerns. Natural gas saw short-term supply apprehensions after Qatar delayed LNG deliveries, whereas power prices exhibited a modest 2-3% downward correction in the second half of the year. China is accelerating grid integration for solar and wind power nationwide, while the US aims to reduce energy import dependence. South Korea’s energy storage and wind turbine sectors benefit from lower generation costs and global renewable supply diversification strategies.
