Oil Soars 7%, AI Data Center Power Costs Surge 30%

Energy and AI sectors face dual shocks from rising oil prices and soaring data center electricity rates

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

US markets showed mixed performance with the Nasdaq dropping 1.2%, while the S&P 500 edged up by 0.4%. The banking and financial sectors fell 0.7% amid concerns over deepening income inequality in the US. Technology stocks weakened, led by Meta's 2.8% decline due to slower-than-expected AI growth. Heightened geopolitical tensions between the US and Iran, alongside monetary policy uncertainty, intensified risk-off sentiment, pushing the 10-year US Treasury yield up by 6 basis points to 4.36%. Overall, investor risk aversion dominated market dynamics.

💡 Analyst Insights
Short-term negative pressure is intensifying on financial stocks as global funds rotate toward consumer staples and essential goods. Korean beneficiaries include BGF Retail, which recorded a 9% sales increase in H1 driven by stable demand for essential products, and Shinsegae Food, with a 23% rise in quarterly net profit fueled by resilient dining out trends. Globally, Walmart stands out as a defensive play, projecting over 5% growth through 2026 despite economic uncertainty. We recommend overweighting defensive sectors in the near term while monitoring for a potential rotation back into financials and growth stocks if US employment and wage data improve or geopolitical risks ease. Key risks include unexpected shifts in Fed policy, sudden global economic downturns, and renewed US-China trade tensions.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

AI infrastructure investment is accelerating, driving rapid expansion in AI-driven storage and data center markets. Samsung Electronics has commenced mass production of next-generation SSDs tailored for AI workloads, while Meta has broken ground on its first mega data center in Canada, investing approximately KRW 2.5 trillion (USD 2 billion). The North American data center market is forecast to grow 18% annually, reaching about KRW 80 trillion this year. However, local community opposition related to power and environmental concerns has resulted in moratoriums on new data center projects in regions such as Hamilton, Ontario, and Prince William County, Virginia. China is addressing AI semiconductor supply issues through government policy shifts that permit limited imports of Nvidia’s H200 AI chips.

💡 Analyst Insights
The AI data center and storage sectors represent a critical growth area for Korean tech companies. Samsung Electronics targets a 35% increase in AI-dedicated SSD sales, and SK Hynix is forecasting a 44% surge in AI server DRAM shipments in 2026. Globally, Nvidia remains the dominant player, expecting over 50% AI data center revenue growth by 2026. Energy Energy · Industry

International oil prices spiked 7% amid renewed US-Iran tensions and increased maritime risk in the Strait of Hormuz, with Brent crude reclaiming the USD 97 per barrel level. US natural gas prices jumped 12% driven by inventory imbalances and reduced shipping volumes across key east-west corridors. In Oregon, heavy data center power demand triggered a 30% electricity rate hike for facilities near Portland. Europe maintained electricity supply stability by bolstering interconnections amidst Ukrainian power disruptions. China continues to emphasize energy security while increasing pressures to expand renewable energy investments.

💡 Analyst Insights
Rising electricity prices and surging oil costs pressure energy-intensive sectors such as steel, petrochemicals, and major power consumers like Samsung SDI and SK Siltron in the short term. Conversely, renewable energy asset holders, notably Korea Electric Power Corporation (KEPCO) and Hyundai Energy Solutions, stand to benefit. In the global arena, NextEra Energy capitalizes on increased power demand from data centers, supporting near-term growth. We advise a cautious stance, reducing exposure to sensitive industries while maintaining holdings in renewable and grid infrastructure stocks. Potential escalation of Middle Eastern geopolitical risks or increased US policy uncertainty could simultaneously push oil and power prices higher. Conversely, if AI infrastructure demand moderates, renewable energy equities may face sharp near-term declines.
