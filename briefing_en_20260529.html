Anthropic Valued at $965 Billion Signals Major AI Capital Influx

Massive AI funding and commercialization spark rebound in Korean IT and semiconductor sectors

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

U.S. markets closed lower yesterday with Nasdaq down 0.7% and S&P 500 down 0.3%, driven by technology sector weakness. Key AI and semiconductor names like MicroStrategy and Micron faced short-term valuation pressures prompting some profit-taking. Investors showed signs of reallocating capital toward dividend-paying stocks amid ongoing uncertainties over macroeconomic conditions and earnings. Expectations of future U.S. interest rate cuts remain but near-term adjustments in risk appetite continue. The Korean won weakened by 0.3%, with Korean bond markets shifting to a wait-and-see stance.

💡 Analyst Insights
The semiconductor and AI-related sectors are undergoing short-term correction due to elevated valuations. Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix still stand to gain from mid-term AI server expansions, but we recommend reducing exposure for now to manage risk. Domestic dividend leaders such as Shinhan Financial Group and Hana Financial Group attract investor attention, supported by improving net interest margins and stable cash flows. Globally, Johnson & Johnson maintains robust performance as a reliable dividend stock. Our tactical approach favors overweighting dividend growth stocks while adopting a more cautious or profit-taking stance on semiconductor holdings. However, geopolitical tensions and potential U.S. earnings shocks remain key downside risks that investors should monitor closely.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

Anthropic, a leading AI startup, completed a massive $65 billion funding round, valuing the company at approximately $965 billion and signaling large-scale commercialization of AI technologies. Major global cloud providers including Amazon Web Services and Cloudflare are heavily investing in machine-network infrastructure to support AI-driven internet traffic. In South Korea, IT giants Naver and Kakao, along with memory semiconductor producers like SK Hynix, stand to benefit from this wave of AI adoption. The global AI market is forecasted to grow at an annual rate exceeding 30%, expanding beyond $500 billion by 2026.
