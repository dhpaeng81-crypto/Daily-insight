AI Bubble Warning and Oil Stability Benefit Hyundai, Samsung

Short-term AI correction points funds to energy and finance large-caps

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

U.S. major indices edged down around 0.5%, with notable fund outflows from telecom and IT large-caps. Meanwhile, value sectors like financials and industrials showed relative strength, exemplified by financial index rising 0.8% versus tech index down 0.6%. The 10-year U.S. Treasury yield slightly increased to 4.31%, reflecting mixed sentiments amid economic slowdown concerns. Emerging markets presented a mixed picture, pressured by a firming dollar index at 104.1 (+0.3%), prompting foreign investors to retreat from risk assets.

💡 Analyst Insights
In the short term, financial and industrial large-caps stand to benefit from sector rotation. Korean firms KB Financial Group, supported by strong retail loan growth and attractive dividends, and Hyundai Motor Company, boosted by robust new vehicle demand and expansions in the U.S. and India, are positioned for near-term earnings momentum. Globally, Morgan Stanley plans to raise its value stock allocation to 20%, signaling capital flows towards financials. The tactical recommendation is to increase exposure to financials and consumer stocks in Q1. Investors should monitor potential volatility from an expanded U.S. yield curve differential and possible rebound in growth stocks that may trigger short-term adjustments.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and IT sectors face growing concerns over overvaluation despite ongoing investment and technological advances. Investment in Nvidia GPU-powered data centers exceeds USD 600 million per megawatt, highlighting the cost intensity. Some AI startups have been found overstating profitability metrics such as Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR). Major tech firms including Google and Anthropic accelerate new product launches like AI glasses and cloud services, but monetization pace lags behind technology adoption. Meanwhile, Korean semiconductor exports, led by Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix, surged over 40% year-over-year through June, providing a temporary tailwind across the sector.

💡 Analyst Insights
Memory semiconductors and related components benefit most from evolving AI technology demand. Samsung Electronics is seeing significant sales growth especially in high-value HBM3 chips, while Naver's cloud AI API revenues are increasing. Globally, Nvidia maintains dominant market share with 80% in GPU and AI infrastructure. The advised strategy is to gradually reduce and hold positions in AI/IT stocks during H1 to crystallize gains amid valuation concerns. Key risks stem from potential AI bubble bursts due to investment overheating and tightening government regulations in labor and data privacy.
Energy Energy · Industry

WTI crude oil prices remain range-bound near USD 77.1 per barrel, down slightly by 0.1%, with no clear directional trend despite U.S.-Iran geopolitical risks and ongoing refinery maintenance worldwide. Natural gas prices fell by 3.05% to USD 3.02 amid waning commodity demand. Domestically, increased government subsidies to expand renewable energy infrastructure, including solar and wind, support stocks like Doosan Enerbility and Hanwha Solutions.
