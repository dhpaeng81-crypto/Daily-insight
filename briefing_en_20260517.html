Energy Energy · Industry

Global oil prices surged nearly 4% to $86 per barrel (Brent) amid escalating Middle East conflict and supply chain disruptions, including potential closures of the strategic Strait of Hormuz. These tensions have reinforced the demand outlook for LNG carriers, driving Korean shipbuilders to secure ₩2.6 trillion in new orders. The expiration of US waivers permitting Russian oil sales adds to medium- and long-term market uncertainties. Concurrently, rising demand for renewable energy and power for data centers and natural gas generation shapes the evolving energy landscape.
