KOSPI Drops 0.8% as AI, Energy Stocks See Volatility Amid Geopolitical Risks

Financial uncertainty fuels sector rotations; energy supply concerns and AI competition intensify

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

The South Korean KOSPI index declined by 0.8%, breaking below the 2600 level to close at 2595 points. U.S. Dow Jones futures opened down 0.4%, pressured by renewed uncertainties over the Iran situation and comments from former President Trump on a possible Iran deal. Defensive consumer discretionary sectors, including retail and semiconductors, underperformed with notable weakness in large retail stocks after a major Korean retailer announced full store closures for the first time in 33 years. The bond market reflected safe-haven demand, with the 3-year government bond yield falling 2 basis points to 3.47%. The USD/KRW exchange rate rose by 6 won to close at 1342, signaling foreign capital outflows.

💡 Analyst Insights
Investors should consider reducing short-term exposure to consumer retail and discretionary sectors, which are vulnerable to weakening domestic consumption amid declining consumer sentiment. Leading Korean retailers Emart and Lotte Shopping are expected to face headwinds from cautious new store investments and softer demand, potentially pressuring their stock prices. In contrast, U.S. tech stocks like Tesla and Nvidia are rallying about 3% due to strong AI and automotive growth prospects, though broad consumer goods weakness remains a challenge. A hold or cautious approach is advisable until signs of a consumer sentiment rebound above the 70-point threshold and quarterly earnings from key large-cap consumer names confirm a recovery. Heightened geopolitical tensions, specifically the risk of renewed Strait of Hormuz disruptions, remain a key downside risk.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and IT sectors exhibited mixed performance amid intensifying competition for ecosystem dominance and early-stage commercialization. Samsung Electronics rose 2.2%, buoyed by news of expanding exports of its AI-specific semiconductors, while Naver climbed 3.1%, supported by advancements in AI voice restoration and cloud services. Chinese competitor DeepSeek's aggressive 75% price cut on its flagship AI model has escalated global price competition. Major international players such as Google and IBM are focusing on enhancing existing platform experiences rather than aggressive market expansion. The domestic Korean AI/IT market is forecast to grow approximately 12% in 2024, with semiconductors, cloud infrastructure, and data center sectors poised as key beneficiaries.

💡 Analyst Insights
Mid-term investors should prioritize Korean semiconductor companies like Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix, alongside data center operator Naver, benefitting from technology advancements in AI voice restoration and aggressive pricing. Samsung’s proprietary AI S-AI chip export expansion could boost operating profit from KRW 8 trillion to KRW 10 trillion next year. Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices rose 1.2% to close at USD 81 per barrel, supported by reports of damage to Russian Black Sea oil facilities and a 2,400-gallon pipeline spill in East Los Angeles, exacerbating supply concerns. Cross-border energy infrastructure attacks between Ukraine and Russia have further underscored supply chain uncertainties. In power markets, Seattle’s introduction of a time-of-use electricity pricing model is expected to increase price volatility. Concurrently, a 10% reduction in energy intensity and renewable energy adoption by 30 local governments are driving stronger carbon emission reduction efforts.
