Oil Near $100, Energy and AI Sectors See Rising Volatility

Oil close to $100 boosts energy; AI and tech stocks face short-term correction with sector rotation underway

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

U.S. equity markets declined, with the Nasdaq down 1.3%, the S&P 500 falling 0.9%, and the Dow Jones dropping 0.7%. Technology stocks led the weakness, whereas cybersecurity, defense, and energy sectors showed relative strength. Rising global inflation and a surge in crude oil prices to $99.5 per barrel (WTI) pressured markets broadly. The 10-year U.S. Treasury yield climbed to 4.45%, contributing to risk-off sentiment in bond markets.

💡 Analyst Insights
Sectors centered on physical infrastructure and public investment—such as energy, defense, and pharmaceuticals—are positioned to benefit in this environment. South Korea’s Hanwha Ocean, a defense and shipbuilding conglomerate, is securing more defense and offshore energy contracts, while SK Innovation is directly gaining from higher crude prices and improved refining margins. Globally, ExxonMobil expects over a 20% rise in Q3 operating profits due to the oil rally. We recommend increasing exposure to energy and defense stocks in the near term. However, investors should remain cautious of potential downside risks if geopolitical tensions ease or if the U.S. Federal Reserve intensifies monetary tightening, which could depress oil prices and reduce defense demand.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and IT sector experienced a short-term pullback despite innovation announcements from major tech firms. Apple unveiled AI-enhanced features in its latest OS and improvements to Siri, but shares face pressure given already high growth expectations (projected 27.1% annual growth). Competition is intensifying, highlighted by Anthropic’s release of the Fable 5 AI model, intensifying battles among AI platform providers. The hardware ecosystem—including AI servers, semiconductors, and cloud infrastructure—as well as budget-friendly AI models, is expanding concurrently.

Energy Energy · Industry

Crude oil prices surged to $99.5 per barrel (WTI) driven by tighter sanctions on Russia and accelerated strategic petroleum reserve (SPR) purchases. The European Union froze its price cap on Russian crude and introduced additional sanctions, heightening supply uncertainties. Concurrently, electricity and gas prices rose by 12% and 8% respectively in the U.S. and Europe, underscoring a firm energy market. Although renewable energy investment discussions continue, current short-term supply-demand imbalances dominate the price dynamics.

💡 Analyst Insights
The rise in oil and gas prices places cost pressures on large Korean industrial users like Korea Electric Power Corporation (KEPCO) and Samsung Heavy Industries, which rely heavily on imported raw materials. Conversely, refiners such as SK Innovation and S-Oil benefit from inventory revaluation gains and robust refining margins, improving their earnings outlook. ExxonMobil has ascended to the world’s second largest energy company by market capitalization amid rising strategic reserve demand and supply chain disruptions. We recommend buying energy and refining stocks for a horizon of up to three months. Investors should monitor for policy shifts, such as accelerated energy transition efforts or reversal of price caps in Europe, which could precipitate a price correction.
