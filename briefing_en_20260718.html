Intel Down 17% in One Month: Long-Term Buy Signal Emerging

Iran-driven oil surge, AI sector clashes, and heightened tech stock volatility dominate markets

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

U.S. major indices showed mixed performance, with the Nasdaq tech-heavy index declining 3.5% over the past month. Intel shares notably dropped 17% during this period, while select semiconductor stocks managed partial recoveries. Soybean futures have been strong, challenging their May peak, contributing to a robust commodities sector. Global bond markets have remained cautious, with the U.S. 10-year Treasury yield fluctuating between 4.2% and 4.3%. Geopolitical tensions stemming from the Iran conflict are reinforcing safe-haven demand globally.

💡 Analyst Insights
Short-term rebounds are probable in semiconductor and commodity sectors. Intel’s 17% correction could precede a doubling in its share price within 6 to 12 months, targeting $90 (approx. 120,000 KRW). Korean semiconductor giants Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix are trading in undervalued zones and stand to benefit from renewed inflows into U.S. tech shares. Globally, Nvidia remains a clear beneficiary of sustained AI demand and semiconductor capex expansion. Recommended investment actions include buying or increasing exposure to Intel, Samsung Electronics, and SK Hynix. However, investors should monitor risks such as prolonged U.S.-China trade conflicts and potential slowdown in AI semiconductor revenue growth.
Tech AI · Tech Trends

The AI and IT sectors face modest valuation adjustments, yet growth momentum persists strongly. Databricks reached an impressive $188 billion valuation, underscoring its prominence as an AI platform innovator and its efforts to reduce costs via open weight AI models. Apple has intensified the global AI technology rivalry by filing a trade secrets lawsuit against OpenAI, exacerbating regulatory and IP tensions. In India, AI-enabled smartphones are proliferating, but this expansion coincides with a 2% contraction in the mid-to-low tier smartphone market, illustrating nuanced regional demand shifts. Korean IT firms are expected to continue their role in reshaping B2B AI and data service value chains.

💡 Analyst Insights
AI service providers and semiconductor memory chip manufacturers are poised for growth. Domestically, Naver’s development of large-scale AI models and LG Energy Solution’s smart factory and AI-driven power management initiatives stand out as promising. Globally, Tesla’s advances in AI robotics and autonomous driving, along with Databricks’ data-centric AI platform momentum, underscore strong international leadership. Investment strategies favor short-term buying and long-term holding of Naver and LG Energy Solution. Potential volatility drivers include Apple-OpenAI litigation, tighter regulatory environments, IP disputes, and slower AI smartphone adoption in emerging markets like India.
Energy Energy · Industry

Oil prices surged to monthly highs around $88 per barrel (+4.5%) due to escalating tensions in Iran disrupting supply chains. The Persian Gulf shipping bottleneck is increasingly severe, with only eight vessels transiting the strait, amplifying global energy supply concerns. Meanwhile, electricity prices in some regions have declined amid expanded renewable energy capacity and increased eco-friendly demand. Nations worldwide are ramping up investments in power grids to bolster energy security. Additionally, extreme heat periods are introducing new challenges to reliable electricity supply stability.
