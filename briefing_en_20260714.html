💡 Analyst Insights
Energy and defense stocks have turned bullish on heightened Middle East geopolitical tensions, likely sustaining short-term gains. Domestically, S-Oil stands out with improved refining margins coupled with geopolitical premium, while Korea Shipbuilding & Offshore Engineering benefits from increased shipments circumventing the Strait of Hormuz. KT&G’s ramp-up in defense exports also makes it attractive over the medium term. Globally, ExxonMobil is poised for earnings upside supported by rising oil prices and new oil transport contracts. Investors should consider increasing allocations to energy and defense sectors, but remain cautious of potential volatility from U.S. strategic petroleum reserve releases, a sharp slowdown in Chinese oil demand, or a peace settlement in the region.

Energy Energy · Industry

Geopolitical risks tied to the potential blockade of Middle Eastern oil shipping routes caused Brent crude to spike 8.5% in one day, pushing Dubai crude close to $93 per barrel. The U.S. signaled military support to safeguard oil flows, while UAE and Oman are developing new crude hubs to bypass the Strait of Hormuz. China increased its crude oil imports by 11% year-on-year in Q2 due to strategic stockpiling, contributing to supply-demand tightness across Asia. Concurrently, LNG prices also climbed, reflecting broader energy supply chain disruptions.
