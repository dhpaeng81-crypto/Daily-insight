8.5 Million Barrels of Oil Pass Strait of Hormuz Amid Supply Concerns

Energy supply risk drives oil prices and sector volatility higher

💹 Finance 🤖 AI · Tech ⚡ Energy 📈 Stocks
Finance Financial Markets

On July 14, South Korea’s main Kospi index fell 0.8% to close near 2,730 points, while the tech-heavy Kosdaq dropped 1.1% to the low 880s. Energy, chemical, and defense stocks rallied strongly, posting gains above 2%, contrasting with notable weakness in IT and consumer sectors. US markets reflected mixed sentiment with the Dow Jones nearly flat (+0.1%) and the Nasdaq down 0.6%, showing deepening pressure on technology shares. Investor caution is fueled by ongoing Middle East oil supply risks, prolonged US interest rate peaks, and global bond market volatility.

The US Energy Department confirmed that 8.5 million barrels of American oil transited the critical Strait of Hormuz in the second week of July. This strategic maritime chokepoint’s stability is vital as it handles a significant portion of global oil shipments. Energy Energy · Industry

According to the US Department of Energy, 8.5 million barrels of US-origin oil transited the Strait of Hormuz during the second week of July. This was accompanied by a 4% one-day surge in oil prices nearing $87 per barrel, driven by ongoing geopolitical instability in the Middle East involving Iran and the UAE. Efforts to diversify oil transport routes through Oman and Saudi Arabia are underway. At the same time, major global energy firms are ramping up investments in carbon capture technologies and alternative energy sources to address sustainability challenges.
